%% ====================================================================
\section{Geometry}

The background is a Randall-Sundrum~I spacetime: a slice of $\mathrm{AdS}_5$
compactified on $S^1/\mathbb{Z}_2$, with a UV brane at $y = 0$ and an IR brane
at $y = \pi r_c$. The metric in conformal coordinates is
\begin{equation}
  ds^2 = (kz)^{-2}\bigl(\eta_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu\,dx^\nu + dz^2\bigr),
  \qquad \eta = \mathrm{diag}(-1,+1,+1,+1),
\end{equation}
with the UV brane at $z = 1/k$ and the IR brane at $z = 1/\LamIR$.

Two inputs fix everything: the AdS curvature $k$ and the IR scale $\LamIR$.
We set $k = \Mpl = 1.2209\times10^{19}$~GeV throughout and scan $\LamIR$
from 500~GeV to 20~TeV. The warp factor is $\varepsilon = \LamIR / k \sim
10^{-15}$. The Higgs is taken to be localized on the IR brane, and we use
$v = 174$~GeV as the electroweak VEV (i.e.\ $\langle H \rangle =
v_{\mathrm{SM}}/\sqrt{2} = 174$~GeV). Backreaction is neglected.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Zero-mode profiles}

Each bulk fermion has a dimensionless mass parameter $c = M_5/k$, which
controls where its zero mode is localized in the extra dimension. The key
quantity is the zero-mode's overlap with the IR brane, since the Higgs
(and therefore the Yukawa interaction) lives there:
\begin{equation}
  f_{\mathrm{IR}}^2(c,\varepsilon)
    = \frac{\tfrac{1}{2} - c}{1 - \varepsilon^{1-2c}}\,.
\end{equation}
For $c > 1/2$ the zero mode is localized near the UV brane and $f_{\mathrm{IR}}$
is exponentially small (light fermion); for $c < 1/2$ it is localized near the
IR brane and $f_{\mathrm{IR}} \sim O(1)$ (heavy fermion). This is the core of
the RS flavor mechanism: the quark mass hierarchy comes from the exponential
sensitivity of $f_{\mathrm{IR}}$ to $c$, not from hierarchical 5D Yukawa
couplings. For example, at $\LamIR = 3$~TeV, $c = 0.72$ gives
$f \sim 4\times 10^{-8}$ while $c = 0.30$ gives $f \sim 0.45$.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Yukawa spurion construction}

The 5D Yukawa matrices $Y_u$ and $Y_d$ are complex $3\times3$ matrices. We
parametrize each via a singular value decomposition:
\begin{equation}
  Y = \texttt{overall\_scale} \times U_{\mathrm{left}}
    \cdot \operatorname{diag}(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3)
    \cdot U_{\mathrm{right}}^\dagger,
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_i > 0$ are ordered light-to-heavy and $U_{\mathrm{left}}$ is a
CKM-like unitary parametrized by three angles $(\theta_{12}, \theta_{13},
\theta_{23})$ and a CP phase $\delta$. The prefactor \texttt{overall\_scale}
is a common real positive number multiplying both $Y_u$ and $Y_d$; it controls
the overall magnitude of the Yukawa entries.

We freeze $U_{\mathrm{right}} = I$
for both sectors, which is a restriction: it means $Y^\dagger Y =
\operatorname{diag}(\sigma^2)$ is automatically diagonal, while
$Y Y^\dagger = U_{\mathrm{left}} \operatorname{diag}(\sigma^2)
U_{\mathrm{left}}^\dagger$ is generally dense. Before the fit starts,
\texttt{overall\_scale} is absorbed into the singular values and set to~1.

The consequence of $U_{\mathrm{right}} = I$ is important: the right-handed
bulk mass matrices $C_u = Y_u^\dagger Y_u$ and $C_d = Y_d^\dagger Y_d$ are
diagonal by construction. All off-diagonal flavor structure lives in the
left-handed matrix $C_Q$, which is the only source of tree-level FCNCs.
This restricts the scan to 15 free parameters (6 singular values +
8 left-rotation angles + 1 overall scale) out of the 37 that a pair of
general $3\times3$ complex matrices would have.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Bulk mass matrices and the role of $r$}

From the Yukawa spurions we build three Hermitian matrices that determine the
bulk localization of the quark zero modes:
\begin{equation}
  C_u = Y_u^\dagger Y_u, \qquad
  C_d = Y_d^\dagger Y_d, \qquad
  C_Q = r\,(Y_u Y_u^\dagger) + (Y_d Y_d^\dagger).
\end{equation}

In the NMFV literature the full form is $C_Q = \alpha_Q\,\mathbf{I} +
\beta_Q\,Y_d Y_d^\dagger + \gamma_Q\,Y_u Y_u^\dagger$, where $\alpha_Q$ is
a flavor-universal bulk mass and $r = \gamma_Q / \beta_Q$. Our scan omits the
$\alpha_Q\,\mathbf{I}$ term; the role of $\alpha_Q$ is played instead by the
nonlinear map described in the next section.

The parameter $r$ controls alignment. When $r = 0$, $C_Q \propto Y_d Y_d^\dagger$,
which can be diagonalized simultaneously with $Y_d$. In that basis the
left-handed overlap matrix $F_Q$ is diagonal, so there are no tree-level FCNCs
in the down sector. As $r$ increases, the $Y_u Y_u^\dagger$ term (which
contains CKM-like rotations relative to $Y_d$) introduces off-diagonal entries
in $C_Q$, misaligning the left-handed quarks from the down mass eigenbasis and
generating tree-level FCNCs.

%% ====================================================================
\section{From spurion eigenvalues to physical bulk masses}

We diagonalize each $C_X$ to get eigenvalues $\lambda_i$ and a rotation
matrix. The rotation for $C_Q$ is physically important: it defines the
transformation from the flavor basis to the bulk-mass eigenbasis.

The eigenvalues of $C_Q$ are non-negative, but they are not directly the
bulk mass parameters $c$ that enter the $f$-factor formula. We need a map
$\lambda \to c$. Because our $C_Q$ has no $\alpha_Q$ term, the eigenvalues
can range from 0 to arbitrarily large values, so we apply a bounded sigmoid:
\begin{equation}
  c = 0.72 - 0.42\,\frac{\lambda}{\lambda + 1}\,.
\end{equation}
This maps $\lambda = 0 \to c = 0.72$ (UV-localized, light), $\lambda = 1
\to c = 0.51$, and $\lambda \to \infty \to c \to 0.30$ (IR-localized, heavy).
The window $[0.30, 0.72]$ is a design choice: $c = 0.30$ gives
$f_{\mathrm{IR}} \sim 0.45$, which forces the top Yukawa singular value to
be $\sim 2.4$ to reproduce $m_t = 172$~GeV (perturbative but above the naive
$O(1)$ expectation).

The sigmoid is an artifact of omitting $\alpha_Q$. In the standard
parametrization, $\alpha_Q$ provides a flavor-universal baseline, and the
eigenvalues of $C_Q$ are directly the bulk masses $c_{Q,i}$ with no need for
a nonlinear map.

\gamma_Q)$ parametrization, the sigmoid would be replaced by a direct

%% ====================================================================
\section{Zero-mode overlaps, basis rotation, and mass matrices}

With the physical bulk masses in hand, we compute the IR-brane overlaps
$F_Q[i] = f_{\mathrm{IR}}(c_Q[i], \varepsilon)$ for the left-handed doublets,
and similarly $F_u$, $F_d$ for the right-handed singlets. We then rotate the
Yukawas into the bulk-mass eigenbasis:
$Y^{\mathrm{bulk}} = \text{rot}_Q^\dagger \cdot Y \cdot \text{rot}_{u,d}$.
This rotation diagonalizes the bulk masses (so we can apply $F$ as a diagonal
matrix), but it does not diagonalize the Yukawas; $Y^{\mathrm{bulk}}$ is
generally a dense $3\times3$ complex matrix.

The effective 4D quark mass matrices are then
\begin{equation}
  M_{u,d} = 2v \cdot \operatorname{diag}(F_Q) \cdot Y_{u,d}^{\mathrm{bulk}}
    \cdot \operatorname{diag}(F_{u,d}),
\end{equation}
where $2v = 348$~GeV $\approx \sqrt{2}\,v_{\mathrm{SM}}$. The 5D Yukawas
in this convention are dimensionless. The physical quark mass for generation
$i$ is roughly $m_i \sim 2v\,F_Q[i]\,Y_{ii}^{\mathrm{bulk}}\,F_{u\text{ or }d}[i]$:
the mass hierarchy comes from the exponential spread in the $F$-factors.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Physical masses, CKM, and the fit}

Each mass matrix is decomposed by SVD:
$M = U_L \operatorname{diag}(m_1, m_2, m_3) U_R^\dagger$, giving the physical
quark masses as singular values. The CKM matrix is $V_{\mathrm{CKM}} =
(U_L^u)^\dagger U_L^d$, from which we extract $|V_{us}|$, $|V_{cb}|$,
$|V_{ub}|$, and the Jarlskog invariant $J$.

The entire chain from Yukawa parameters to masses and CKM is wrapped in a
Trust Region Reflective optimizer, which adjusts 14 free parameters (6
log-singular-values $+$ 8 left-rotation angles) to match the observed quark
masses and CKM elements. The residual is a 10-component vector: 6 log-mass
ratios plus 4 fractional CKM deviations. We define the fit score as the RMS
of these 10 residuals; points with score $> 0.1$ (roughly $> 10\%$ average
deviation) are discarded.

The target masses ($m_u = 1.3$~MeV, $m_c = 620$~MeV, $m_t = 172$~GeV,
$m_d = 2.8$~MeV, $m_s = 57$~MeV, $m_b = 2.86$~GeV) are repo-owned values
chosen for scan stability, not $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ masses at a consistent
scale. This is an uncontrolled systematic.

\section{The scan}

With the physics pipeline established (Yukawas $\to$ bulk masses $\to$
overlaps $\to$ mass matrices $\to$ fit), we can describe the scan itself. It
sweeps three parameters on a grid:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
Parameter & Grid & Range & Points \\
\midrule
$r$ & log-uniform & 0.02--2.0 & 100 \\
\texttt{overall\_scale} & linear & 1.5--6.0 & 20 \\
$\LamIR$ & log-uniform & 500--20\,000~GeV & 50 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

$r$ controls the degree of alignment in $C_Q$ (previous sections).
\texttt{overall\_scale} sets the overall Yukawa magnitude. Different values
lead to different internal Yukawa structures even after the fitter re-optimizes
to hit the same quark masses: if \texttt{overall\_scale} is large, all Yukawa
entries are large and the fitter has to make the singular values small to
compensate; if it is small, the singular values have to be large. These
different configurations produce different off-diagonal entries in the mass
basis and therefore different FCNC predictions, even though the diagonal masses
come out the same. Over the scan range 1.5 to 6.0, the largest singular value
(top-like) runs from $\sim 1.5$ to $\sim 6$ and the smallest (up-like) from
$\sim 0.005$ to $\sim 0.02$. At the high end the top Yukawa is well above the
naive $O(1)$ expectation. $\LamIR$ sets the KK scale and therefore the
$1/\Mkk^2$ suppression of all Wilson coefficients.

Total: $100 \times 20 \times 50 = 100{,}000$ points, each fitted independently
from the same deterministic seed.

At scan time several quantities are held fixed. The KK-mass convention is
$\xi_{\mathrm{KK}} = 1.0$, meaning $\Mkk = \LamIR$ in the Wilson coefficient
propagator (corrected post-hoc at plotting time; see below). The enhanced
KK-gluon coupling is $\gs = 3.0$, the one-loop estimate from
Gedalia et al.\ (2009).

\section{KK-gluon couplings in the mass basis}

After the fit converges we have the diagonal overlaps $F_Q$, $F_u$, $F_d$ and
the left/right rotation matrices $U_L^{u,d}$, $U_R^{u,d}$ from the SVD. The
first KK-gluon excitation couples to zero-mode quarks through their IR-brane
overlap; in the bulk-mass eigenbasis this coupling is diagonal
($\gs \operatorname{diag}(F^2)$), but in the physical mass basis it picks up
off-diagonal entries from the rotations:
\begin{equation}
  g_{L}^{\mathrm{down}}[i,j] = \gs \cdot
    \bigl(U_L^{d\,\dagger} \operatorname{diag}(F_Q^2)\, U_L^d\bigr)_{ij},
\end{equation}
and analogously for $g_R^{\mathrm{down}}$ (using $U_R^d$, $F_d$),
$g_L^{\mathrm{up}}$ (using $U_L^u$, $F_Q$), and $g_R^{\mathrm{up}}$ (using
$U_R^u$, $F_u$). Note that $F_Q$ enters both LH couplings because $u_L$ and
$d_L$ share the same $SU(2)$ doublet.

These coupling matrices are Hermitian. Their off-diagonal entries, which are
generically complex, drive flavor-changing neutral currents. Since $\gs$
enters all Wilson coefficients as ${\gs}^2$, the choice between the tree-level
value ($\gs \sim 6$) and the one-loop value ($\gs = 3$) is a factor of 4 in
all constraint ratios.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Wilson coefficients}

Integrating out the KK-gluon at tree level at the scale $\mu = \Mkk$ gives
four $\Delta F = 2$ operators in the BMU basis (Buras, Misiak, Urban,
hep-ph/0005183):
\begin{align}
  C_1^{\mathrm{VLL}} &= \frac{(g_L[i,j])^2}{6\,\Mkk^2}\,, \qquad
  C_1^{\mathrm{VRR}} = \frac{(g_R[i,j])^2}{6\,\Mkk^2}\,, \\[4pt]
  C_4^{\mathrm{LR}} &= -\frac{g_L[i,j]\,g_R[i,j]}{\Mkk^2}\,, \qquad
  C_5^{\mathrm{LR}} = \frac{g_L[i,j]\,g_R[i,j]}{3\,\Mkk^2}\,.
\end{align}
The coefficients $1/6$, $-1$, $+1/3$ come from the color algebra of
$t$-channel KK-gluon exchange after Fierz rearrangement. Only VLL, VRR, and
LR operators appear at tree level (no SLL/SRR, which would require scalar
currents). The Wilson coefficients are complex; their imaginary parts carry
CP violation and are essential for the $\epsilon_K$ constraint.

The five meson systems and their quark-pair indices are: $\epsilon_K$ and
$\Delta M_K$ use $(d,s)$; $B_d$ uses $(d,b)$; $B_s$ uses $(s,b)$; $D^0$
uses $(u,c)$. We include only the first KK-gluon mode (higher modes are a
$\sim 10$--$20\%$ correction) and use $\Mkk = \LamIR$ as both the
propagator mass and matching scale.


%% ====================================================================
\section{QCD running}

The Wilson coefficients are matched at $\Mkk$ (multi-TeV) and the hadronic
matrix elements are evaluated at $\mu_{\mathrm{had}} = 2$~GeV, so we evolve
the coefficients down using leading-log QCD RG equations. The VLL and VRR
operators run multiplicatively with anomalous dimension $\gamma = 4$. The LR
operators $C_4$ and $C_5$ mix via the $2\times2$ anomalous dimension matrix
\begin{equation}
  \gamma_{\mathrm{LR}} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & -4 \\ -16/3 & -28/3 \end{pmatrix}.
\end{equation}
The dominant eigenvalue is large and positive, giving a $3$--$5\times$
enhancement of the LR operators at low scales. Combined with the chiral
enhancement factor $r_\chi = (m_K/(m_s + m_d))^2 \approx 25$ in the kaon
matrix elements, this is why $\epsilon_K$ is the most powerful constraint.

The running crosses one flavor threshold at $m_b = 4.18$~GeV ($n_f = 5 \to 4$);
$m_c = 1.27$~GeV is below $\mu_{\mathrm{had}}$. We use one-loop $\alpha_s$
running with $\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1179$ and continuous threshold matching.


%% ====================================================================
\section{Hadronic matrix elements and observables}

The evolved Wilson coefficients at $\mu_{\mathrm{had}} = 2$~GeV are
contracted with hadronic matrix elements to get the NP mixing amplitude:
\begin{equation}
  M_{12}^{\mathrm{NP}} = C_1^{\mathrm{VLL}} \cdot \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{VLL}}
    + C_1^{\mathrm{VRR}} \cdot \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{VLL}}
    + C_4^{\mathrm{LR}} \cdot \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{LR4}}
    + C_5^{\mathrm{LR}} \cdot \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{LR5}},
\end{equation}
where VRR and VLL share the same matrix element by parity. The matrix
elements are
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{VLL}} = \tfrac{2}{3} f_P^2 m_P B_1, \qquad
  \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{LR4}} = \bigl(\tfrac{r_\chi}{6} + \tfrac{1}{4}\bigr)
    f_P^2 m_P B_4, \qquad
  \mathrm{ME}_{\mathrm{LR5}} = \bigl(\tfrac{r_\chi}{2} + \tfrac{1}{12}\bigr)
    f_P^2 m_P B_5,
\end{equation}
with $r_\chi = (m_P / (m_{q_1} + m_{q_2}))^2$. Here $M_{12}$ is the full
off-diagonal element $\langle\bar{P}|H_{\mathrm{eff}}|P\rangle$ in GeV (no
$1/(2m_P)$ normalization), so $\Delta m = 2|M_{12}|$.

Hadronic inputs (decay constants, bag parameters, quark masses) come from
FLAG~2024 and PDG~2024, with the exception of the $D^0$ LR bag parameters
$B_4$, $B_5$ which are estimated at 1.0. Full input tables are in the long
document.

\section{Acceptance criteria}

Five meson systems are tested: $\epsilon_K$, $\Delta M_K$, $\Delta M_{B_d}$,
$\Delta M_{B_s}$, $\Delta M_{D^0}$. A point passes only if all five satisfy
$\text{ratio} \le 1$.

For $\epsilon_K$, the observable is
$\epsilon_K^{\mathrm{NP}} = |\kappa_\epsilon / (\sqrt{2}\,\Delta m_K)| \cdot
|\mathrm{Im}(M_{12}^{\mathrm{NP}})|$
with $\kappa_\epsilon = 0.94$, and the budget is the exp--SM gap:
$|2.228\times10^{-3} - 1.81\times10^{-3}| = 4.18\times10^{-4}$.
The absolute value discards the sign of the NP phase (no constructive/destructive
interference with SM).

For $\Delta M_K$, the budget is $\Delta m_K^{\mathrm{exp}} / 2$ (the SM
prediction is long-distance dominated and unreliable, so the entire
experimental value is treated as room for NP).

For $B_d$ and $B_s$, the budget is
$\max(\Delta m^{\mathrm{exp}}/2,\; |\Delta m^{\mathrm{exp}} -
\Delta m^{\mathrm{SM}}|/2)$, which in practice is dominated by
$\Delta m^{\mathrm{exp}}/2$. For $D^0$ it is simply
$\Delta m^{\mathrm{exp}}/2$.

\section{Post-processing and figures}

Points with fit score $> 0.1$ or failed convergence are discarded.

Because the scan used $\Mkk = \LamIR$ as the propagator mass, but the
physical first KK-gluon mass is $m_g^{(1)} = \xi_{\mathrm{kk}} \LamIR$ with
$\xi_{\mathrm{kk}} = 2.4487$ (the first gauge-sector Bessel root), the
plotting script relabels the axis to $m_g^{(1)}$ and divides all ratios by
$\xi_{\mathrm{kk}}^2$ to correct the propagator. This does not correct the
QCD running start scale (see the TODO above).

At each $(r, \Mkk)$ grid point there are up to 20 \texttt{overall\_scale}
values. The plotter picks the single \texttt{overall\_scale} that minimizes
$\max(\text{all 5 ratios})$, so that all five contour lines in Figure~1 come
from one physically consistent MFV point.

Both figures are best-case envelopes: the fitter found the most favorable
Yukawa structure at each grid point, and the plotter picked the most favorable
\texttt{overall\_scale}. The contour shows the lowest $\Mkk$ at which any
fitted point survives. This is an existence bound, not a statement about
typical NMFV behavior.

\section{Summary of assumptions}

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item RS1 geometry with $k = \Mpl$, Higgs on the IR brane, no backreaction.
  \item Yukawas parametrized via SVD with $U_{\mathrm{right}} = I$ (15 free
    parameters). Only $C_Q$ has off-diagonal structure.
  \item $C_Q = r\,Y_u Y_u^\dagger + Y_d Y_d^\dagger$, no $\alpha_Q$ term.
    Eigenvalues mapped to $c \in [0.30, 0.72]$ via a sigmoid (artifact of
    omitting $\alpha_Q$).
  \item 14-parameter TRF fit, 120 evaluations, deterministic seed. Best-case
    optimization, not random sampling.
  \item $\gs = 3.0$ (one-loop), entering as ${\gs}^2$ in all Wilson
    coefficients. Factor of 9 relative to perturbative $g_s$.
  \item Tree-level matching at $\Mkk = \LamIR$, first KK-gluon mode only.
  \item LO anomalous dimensions, one-loop $\alpha_s$,
    $\mu_{\mathrm{had}} = 2$~GeV.
  \item FLAG 2024 / PDG 2024 hadronic inputs. $D^0$ $B_{4,5}$ estimated at
    1.0.
  \item $\epsilon_K$ budget: exp--SM gap. $\Delta M_K$: exp/2. $B_{d,s}$:
    $\max(\text{exp}/2, |\text{exp}-\text{SM}|/2)$. $D^0$: exp/2.
  \item Publication axis: $m_g^{(1)} = 2.4487\,\LamIR$ with ratio
    $/\xi_{\mathrm{kk}}^2$ correction.
  \item Figures show an existence bound (best-case envelope), not typical NMFV
    behavior.
  \item Fit targets are repo-owned effective masses, not consistent
    $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$.
\end{enumerate}

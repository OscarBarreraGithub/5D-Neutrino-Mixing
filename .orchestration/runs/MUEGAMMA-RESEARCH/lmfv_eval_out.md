1. **Perez-Randall LMFV**: spurion `(Y_N Y_N†)_eμ`; ~3 `Y_N` eigenvalues + PMNS + `M_KK` + NDA `C`; high predictivity; **does not fully avoid** brane-Higgs counterterm ambiguity; needs existing `Y_N`, PMNS, `M_KK`; predicts `BR ~ 4e-8 |S_N,eμ|^2 (3 TeV/M_KK)^4`.
2. **Chen-Yu / 0804.2503 lepton MFV**: spurions `Y_e`-aligned bulk masses plus neutrino spurions; adds alignment coefficients and non-anarchic/mild-hierarchy assumptions; good motivation but less direct prediction; mostly removes leading charged cubic; needs full aligned `c_L/c_E`, `Y_e`, neutrino sector; structure is residual neutrino/MFV-suppressed LFV.
3. **Clean anarchic charged leptons**: spurion `f_L (Y_E Y_E† Y_E) f_E` plus gauge floor; many scan parameters/phases; predictive only as a band; brane-Higgs treatment is prescription-dependent; needs full complex `Y_E`, `U_L/U_R`, `c_L/c_E`, Higgs/custodial choices.
4. **5D MFV / gauged flavor variants**: Yukawas control bulk masses or symmetry breaking; potentially elegant, but more model-specific and not repo-ready; needs new flavor symmetry data/coefficient choices; not cleaner for a single `mu->e gamma` constraint.
5. Decision: **LMFV remains the cleanest choice** for this framework; Chen-Yu is the closest competitor but is not cleaner overall because it buys UV/alignment advantages with extra structural assumptions and weaker repo-level implementability.
6. Recommended choice: keep Perez-Randall LMFV as primary; inputs are `Y_N` or seesaw-derived `Y_N`, PMNS, `M_KK`, and documented `C`; output is the neutrino-spurion dipole bound/rate, explicitly labeled NDA and model-specific.

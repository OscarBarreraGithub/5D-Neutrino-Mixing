## Scope

R02-I2 flagged that the literal arrays in
`quarkConstraints/benchmarks.py::default_spurion_seed` (e.g.
`up_singular_values = [0.1434…, 0.3337…, 1.2395…]`,
`up_left.theta12 = -2.4041…`) carry no in-source pointer to their
provenance. The re-derivation against PDG-2024 MS-bar quark masses is
recorded in `docs/phase_logs/phase2_h4_impl.md`, and the values are pinned
by `tests/test_quark_fit.py` — but a reader of `benchmarks.py` alone
would see them as magic numbers.

and §C entry `C13 — Spurion-seed provenance comment (R02-I2)`); C04
already added a supplementary docstring cross-reference in

## Implementation

Single edit at `quarkConstraints/benchmarks.py` inside
`def default_spurion_seed() -> SpurionSeed:`:

1. Extended the existing one-line docstring to a multi-line block that
   states the literals were re-derived against PDG-2024 MS-bar quark
   masses, points to `docs/phase_logs/phase2_h4_impl.md` for the
   re-derivation details and audit log, and notes that
   `tests/test_quark_fit.py` pins the values.
2. Added a 3-line `#`-comment immediately above the `return
   SpurionSeed(` literal block restating the same provenance pointer in
   the exact short form requested by the dispatch brief.

Both pointers appear inside the function body (not floating above the
`def` line) so that a reader inspecting the literals via `grep`,
syntax-jump, or AST tooling lands on the provenance text without needing
to scroll up past the signature.

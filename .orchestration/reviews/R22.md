**LaTeX normalizer spot-check (regex set)**:
- `applySharedRules:115` `value.replace(/\s*(?<![<-])->\s*/g, ' \\to ')` — the `(?<![<-])` lookbehind prevents `<->` (bidirectional) and `-->`/`->>` from being eaten by the unidirectional rule; the next line (`:116`) handles `<->`/`<-->` separately into `\leftrightarrow`. Correct.
- `applyMultiCharSubAndSup:156-166` `out.replace(/([_^])\(([^()]*)\)/g, ...)` — parenthesised sub/super first, then `/([_^])([A-Za-z][A-Za-z]+)(?![A-Za-z{}])/` for ≥2-letter alphabetic indices. Single-character (e.g. `_b`) and already-braced (`_{...}`) are explicitly skipped by the negative-lookahead `(?![A-Za-z{}])`. Correct on the K020 / d_Hg / |V_cb| / A_FB / G^(1) examples called out in the commit body.

The accompanying `PROSE_KEEP_WORDS` set (`:240-265`) holds 50+ English physics terms (`mixing`, `inclusive`, `radiative`, `branching`, ...) so a token like `mixing` is kept as text, not flushed into a math span.

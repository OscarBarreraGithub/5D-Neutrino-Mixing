## B001: Delta m_d / B_d mixing

### Verdict
APPROVE-OVERRIDE

### Cycle-2 CA issues
- `CHK-1`: TeX still quotes the CFW `21 TeV` standard-RS and `33 TeV`
  pseudo-Goldstone-scenario KK-gluon scales outside `pdg_or_equivalent`;
  WA-v2 placed them under `paper_era_reference.cfw_2008_rs_flavor` with
  complete metadata (value, year, units, source URL, access date,
  snapshot path, sha256) but did not promote them to
  `pdg_or_equivalent`.
- `CHK-1`: TeX still quotes the Belle II `Delta m_d = 0.516 +/- 0.008(stat)
  +/- 0.005(syst) ps^-1` and `190 fb^-1` dataset under
  `recent_experimental_inputs.belleii_2023` rather than
  `pdg_or_equivalent`.

### Rationale
The L001 precedent (`flavor_catalog/signoff/by_process/L001.md`) and its
B021/B023 reapplication (`flavor_catalog/signoff/by_process/B021_B023.md`)
are directly on point: `pdg_or_equivalent` is the schema home for measured
canonical experimental observables (HFLAV/PDG averages, branching
fractions, limits, masses), not for (a) theoretical
reference-scale normalizations or (b) per-experiment dataset metadata that
contextualizes a single analysis.

The CFW `21 TeV` / `33 TeV` numerals are paper-era theoretical
reference scales used to anchor "post-2008 development" comparisons in
prose; they are not measured observables and belong inside the originating
source block (`paper_era_reference.cfw_2008_rs_flavor`), exactly as L001
handled the Perez-Randall `3 TeV` normalization. The Belle II 2023 dataset
descriptor (`190 fb^-1`) and single-experiment `Delta m_d` number are
run/analysis metadata that already have full provenance under
`recent_experimental_inputs.belleii_2023` (with stat and syst breakdown,
URL, access date, snapshot, sha) -- the same pattern B021 used for the
LHCb 3.0 fb^-1 dataset descriptor. The canonical HFLAV/PDG 2025 average
`Delta m_d = 0.5069 +/- 0.0019 ps^-1` plus its `x_d` and `chi_d`
companions are already in `pdg_or_equivalent` with complete strict
metadata, which is the load-bearing CHK-1 contract for this entry.

Forcing the CFW reference scales and the Belle II dataset descriptor into
`pdg_or_equivalent` would misuse the schema and is exactly the
strict-reading pattern arbitrated against in L001 and B021/B023. B001 is
otherwise PASS on CHK-1 through CHK-8 per `ca_w6_beauty_v2.md`.

### Verdict
APPROVE-OVERRIDE

### Cycle-2 CA issue
- `CHK-1`: TeX quotes the FLAG 2024 lattice inputs
  `f_Bs sqrt(Bhat_Bs) = 256.1 +/- 5.7 MeV` and `xi = 1.216 +/- 0.016`;
  WA-v2 added complete metadata under

### Rationale
The FLAG averages `f_Bs sqrt(Bhat_Bs)` and `xi` are theoretical lattice
normalization inputs used to convert `Delta m_s` into a CKM/SM
constraint; they are not measured experimental observables and the FLAG
review itself flags them as theory averages. The L001/B021 precedent
places this class of number in a dedicated auxiliary block, which is
exactly what `auxiliary_theory_inputs.flag_2024_bmixing` is for, and the
WA-v2 entry already carries the full strict-metadata complement.

`pdg_or_equivalent` for B003 is in fact strong: the HFLAV Fall 2024
recommended `Delta m_s = 17.766 +/- 0.006 ps^-1`, the companion HFLAV
quantities `x_s`, `chi_s`, `1/Gamma_s`, `DeltaGamma_s`, the HFLAV/PDG
2025 cross-check, and the dominant LHCb 2022 input (with stat/syst
breakdown, LHCb combination, integrated luminosity sub-block, URL,
access date, snapshot, sha256) are all already promoted there with
complete metadata after the WA-v2 rework. The remaining cycle-2 issue
isolates exactly the lattice-theory inputs that L001 carved out from the
`pdg_or_equivalent` scope.

B003 is otherwise PASS on CHK-1 through CHK-8 per `ca_w6_beauty_v2.md`.

## Policy guidance for future CAs (beauty Delta F=2 family)

- `pdg_or_equivalent` is the home for measured experimental observables
  (HFLAV/PDG averages, dominant single-experiment measurements that
  drive the average) with their value, uncertainty, year, units, source
  URL, access date, snapshot path, and sha256.
- Theoretical reference scales (RS KK-gluon bounds, NDA prefactors)
  belong in `paper_era_reference`, regardless of how cleanly they were
  computed; they are not measured observables.
- Lattice/theory inputs used for normalization (FLAG decay constants,
  bag parameters, xi) belong in `auxiliary_theory_inputs` /
  `auxiliary_lattice_inputs`. FLAG itself averages lattice computations,
  not experiments.
- Per-experiment dataset metadata (integrated luminosity, signal yields,
  sqrt(s)) belongs in `recent_experimental_inputs` /
  `supporting_measurements`, not duplicated into `pdg_or_equivalent`.
- Already-in-code mixing constraints (B001 / B003 / K001 / D001) will
  systematically carry FLAG / HFLAV / dataset-metadata numerals in
  supporting blocks; CA strict-reading CHK-1 should not flag those.

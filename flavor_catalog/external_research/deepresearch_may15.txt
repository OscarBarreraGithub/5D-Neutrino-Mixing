Open questions and limitations
This catalog is deliberately family-complete rather than per-bin exhaustive. For giant observable blocks —
the canonical example is the standard suite of q 2 -binned B → K ∗ μ+ μ− angular observables — the table
identifies the block, its present experimental status, the RS operator mapping, and the source from which
the full bin-by-bin numerical content should be imported into a later machine-readable fit. That is the right
granularity for a modern RS analysis plan, even though it is not a substitute for the final covariance-matrix
implementation. (hflav.web.cern.ch)
A second limitation is that some secondary electroweak and direct-search entries are inherently
benchmark dependent. For instance, there is no single universal “KK-gluon mass limit” independent of
width, localization pattern, or decay branching fractions; similarly, the usefulness of CLFV, LFU, Higgs-LFV,
and Z -LFV observables depends strongly on whether the model includes warped bulk leptons. I have
therefore marked these rows accordingly and emphasized where they become first-tier only in specific RS
variants. (annualreviews.org)

Title: The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs
Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
Submitted: 2008-04-14; last revised: 2008-04-24, version v2
Journal reference: JHEP 0809:008, 2008
Related DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.0804.1954

Relevant abstract excerpt:

The paper studies the flavor structure of 5D warped models that provide a
dual description of a composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs. It re-examines flavor
constraints on the mass scale of new physics in standard Randall-Sundrum-type
scenarios and finds that the KK gluon mass should generically be heavier than
about 21 TeV. In the pseudo-Goldstone scenario, new contributions to flavor
violation are suppressed by the RS-GIM mechanism but enhance flavor-violation
amplitudes; together with gauge-sized Yukawa couplings, the KK gluon mass is
at least about 33 TeV. The abstract also notes that bounds can be stronger or
weaker depending on the gluon boundary kinetic term and that fully anarchic
warped flavor may require partial flavor symmetries.

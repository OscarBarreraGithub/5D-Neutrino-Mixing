Title: Direct CP Violation in K -> mu^+ mu^-
Authors: Giancarlo D'Ambrosio, Teppei Kitahara
Submitted: 2017-07-21; last revised: 2017-11-15
Journal reference: Phys. Rev. Lett. 119 (2017) 201802
DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.119.201802

Abstract excerpt:
A rare decay K_L -> mu^+ mu^- has been measured precisely, while a rare decay
K_S -> mu^+ mu^- will be observed by an upgrade of the LHCb experiment. Although both
processes are almost CP-conserving decays, we point out that an interference contribution
between K_L and K_S in the kaon beam emerges from a genuine direct CP violation. It is
found that the interference contribution can change K_S -> mu^+ mu^- standard-model
predictions at O(60%). We also stress that an unknown sign of A(K_L -> gamma gamma)
can be determined by a measurement of the interference, which can much reduce a theoretical
uncertainty of B(K_L -> mu^+ mu^-). We also investigate the interference in a new physics
model, where the epsilon'_K / epsilon_K tension is explained by an additional Z-penguin
contribution.

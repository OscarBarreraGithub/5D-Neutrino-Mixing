Abstract excerpt:
A simultaneous analysis of the B+ -> K+ l+l- and B0 -> K*0 l+l- decays is
performed to test muon-electron universality in two ranges of q^2. The
measurement uses beauty meson decays produced in pp collisions collected with
the LHCb detector between 2011 and 2018, corresponding to 9 fb^{-1}. Each of
the four lepton-universality measurements reported is either first in the
given q^2 interval or supersedes previous LHCb measurements. The results are
compatible with Standard Model predictions.

Relevant table excerpt from arXiv TeX source results.tex:
Measured values of R_K and R_K* in low-q^2 and central-q^2 regions:
low-q^2 R_K = 0.994^{+0.090}_{-0.082}^{+0.029}_{-0.027}
central-q^2 R_K = 0.949^{+0.042}_{-0.041}^{+0.022}_{-0.022}
low-q^2 R_K* = 0.927^{+0.093}_{-0.087}^{+0.036}_{-0.035}
central-q^2 R_K* = 1.027^{+0.072}_{-0.068}^{+0.027}_{-0.026}

Conclusion excerpt from arXiv TeX source:
The first uncertainty in each row is statistical and the second systematic.
Each of these four measured relative decay rates is compatible with SM
predictions; their combined compatibility with the SM agrees with the SM
prediction at 0.2 standard deviations. The results supersede previous LHCb
measurements of R_K and R_K*.

Relevant arXiv abstract excerpt:
The paper studies warped-geometry lepton minimal flavor violation. It states
that charged-lepton flavor-changing processes are suppressed when the overall
neutrino-Yukawa scale is slightly smaller than one in units of the curvature,
allowing a cutoff of 10 TeV and 3 TeV Kaluza-Klein masses.

Relevant TeX-source excerpt from section "mu -> e gamma":
The source writes the paper-era experimental bound as

  BR(mu -> e gamma) <= 1.2 x 10^-11.

It then estimates, for an IR-brane Higgs,

  BR(mu -> e gamma)^IR ~ 4 x 10^-8
    x (Ybar_N Ybar_N^\dagger)_{12}^2
    x (3 TeV / M_KK)^4.

The same section translates this into an order-of-magnitude coefficient
0.02 for the neutrino-Yukawa product bound and interprets it as requiring
Y_N k less than or of order 0.1 for the most natural models.

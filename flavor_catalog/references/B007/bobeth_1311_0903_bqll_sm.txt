Title: B_{s,d} -> l+ l- in the Standard Model with Reduced Theoretical Uncertainty
Authors: Christoph Bobeth, Martin Gorbahn, Thomas Hermann, Mikolaj Misiak,
Emmanuel Stamou, Matthias Steinhauser

Minimal extract for B007.

Abstract:
  The paper combines O(alpha_em) and O(alpha_s^2) corrections to
  Bs,d -> l+ l- and presents updated Standard Model branching-ratio
  predictions.  The inclusion of these corrections removes major
  theoretical uncertainties of perturbative origin.

Relevant theory statements:
  These flavor-changing neutral-current decays are generated first at
  one-loop level through W-box and Z-penguin diagrams.  Their branching
  ratios undergo additional helicity suppression by m_l^2 / M_Bq^2.
  The branching-ratio formula depends on the lepton mass, the Bq-meson
  mass, and the decay constant f_Bq.  Operators neglected in the SM may
  matter in beyond-SM theories.

Electron-mode numerical predictions from Eq. (7):
  B_se x 10^14 = 8.54 +- 0.55.
  B_de x 10^15 = 2.48 +- 0.21.

Muon/tau companion predictions from the same equation:
  B_smu x 10^9 = 3.65 +- 0.23.
  B_stau x 10^7 = 7.73 +- 0.49.
  B_dmu x 10^10 = 1.06 +- 0.09.
  B_dtau x 10^8 = 2.22 +- 0.19.

Error-budget context:
  The main parametric uncertainties come from f_Bq and CKM angles.
  Non-parametric uncertainties are estimated at about 1.5% of the
  branching ratios, compared to about 8% before these calculations.

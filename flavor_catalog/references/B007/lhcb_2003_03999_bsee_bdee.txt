Title: Search for the rare decays B^0_s -> e+e- and B^0 -> e+e-
Authors: LHCb Collaboration, R. Aaij et al.
Publication: Phys. Rev. Lett. 124 (2020) 211802

Minimal extract for B007.

Abstract/result:
  A search for Bs0 -> e+ e- and B0 -> e+ e- is performed using LHCb
  proton-proton data at center-of-mass energies of 7, 8, and 13 TeV,
  corresponding to integrated luminosities of 1, 2, and 2 fb^-1.
  No signal is observed.

  Assuming no contribution from B0 -> e+e- decays:
    B(Bs0 -> e+e-) < 9.4 (11.2) x 10^-9 at 90 (95)% CL.

  Assuming no contribution from Bs0 -> e+e- decays:
    B(B0 -> e+e-) < 2.5 (3.0) x 10^-9 at 90 (95)% CL.

Context from the paper introduction:
  Standard Model predictions quoted by LHCb, calculated from Ref. [9]
  and neglecting QED corrections expected at the percent level:
    B(Bs0 -> e+e-) = (8.60 +- 0.36) x 10^-14.
    B(B0 -> e+e-) = (2.41 +- 0.13) x 10^-15.

  With contributions beyond the SM, these branching fractions could be
  significantly larger and close to then-current experimental bounds.
  The previous CDF bounds quoted by LHCb were:
    B(Bs0 -> e+e-) < 2.8 x 10^-7 at 90% CL.
    B(B0 -> e+e-) < 8.3 x 10^-8 at 90% CL.

Conclusion/context:
  The search uses a total integrated luminosity of 5 fb^-1.  Assuming
  SM-like CP-odd (CP-even) Bs0 -> e+e- decays changes the quoted Bs0
  limit by +2.4% (-2.4%).  The limits improve the previous values by
  more than one order of magnitude and constrain contributions beyond
  the SM, for example scalar and pseudoscalar currents.

Title: In Pursuit of New Physics with B^0_{s,d}->l+l-
Authors: Robert Fleischer, Ruben Jaarsma, Gilberto Tetlalmatzi-Xolocotzi

Minimal extract for B007.

Abstract/context:
  Leptonic rare decays of B^0_{s,d} mesons offer a powerful tool to search
  for physics beyond the Standard Model.  The paper discusses the current
  status of B_s^0 -> mu+mu- and explores space for new-physics effects in
  other B^0_{s,d} -> l+l- decays in a scenario with flavour-universal
  Wilson coefficients of the relevant four-fermion operators.

Electron-mode relevance:
  The abstract states that B_s^0 -> tau+tau- and B_s^0 -> e+e- had received
  less attention; at the time, CDF's B_s^0 -> e+e- upper bound was
  2.8 x 10^-7 at 90% CL.  In the scenario considered, new-physics effects
  in B_s^0 -> e+e- can be strongly enhanced by m_mu/m_e, and a similar
  feature arises in B_d^0 -> e+e-.  The authors therefore motivate searches
  for B^0_{s,d} -> e+e- at the LHC and Belle II.

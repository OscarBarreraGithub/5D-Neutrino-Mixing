Title: B Meson Anomalies in a Pati-Salam Model within the Randall-Sundrum Background
Updated: 2018-07-04T07:10:39Z
Published: 2018-01-22T19:00:01Z
Primary category: hep-ph
Journal reference: Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 011801 (2018)
DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.121.011801
Authors: Monika Blanke; Andreas Crivellin

Abstract:
The paper studies a Pati-Salam model implemented in a five-dimensional
Randall-Sundrum background. It states that, in conventional Pati-Salam models,
the symmetry-breaking scale and vector-leptoquark mass are stringently
constrained by K_L -> mu e and K -> pi mu e, while adding vector-like fermions
can lower the scale to the TeV range. In the RS realization, the Pati-Salam
symmetry is broken by boundary conditions on the fifth dimension, and the
massive vector leptoquark has the same mass scale as the vector-like fermions
and other resonances. The paper studies this setup in relation to hints of
lepton-flavour universality violation in semileptonic B decays.

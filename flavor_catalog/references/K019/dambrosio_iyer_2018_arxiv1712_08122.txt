Title: Flavour issues in warped custodial models: B anomalies and rare K decays
Updated: 2018-10-19T12:17:57Z
Published: 2017-12-21T17:59:52Z
Primary category: hep-ph
DOI: 10.1140/epjc/s10052-018-5915-9
Authors: Giancarlo D'Ambrosio; Abhishek M. Iyer

Abstract:
The paper explores the flavour structure of custodial Randall-Sundrum models
in the context of deviations in B-meson decays. It states that the anomalies
in b -> s l l processes can be fit with partially composite second-generation
leptons and third-generation quarks, establishes correlations with parameter
space consistent with neutral-current flavour anomalies, and obtains
predictions for rare K decays. The authors discuss two scenarios for the
source of lepton non-universality and note that rare K-decay predictions can
serve as discriminants. They also comment on implications of lepton-sector
flavour violation and give an example using bulk leptonic MFV to alleviate
constraints.

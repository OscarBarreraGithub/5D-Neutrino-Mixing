Title: Natural Neutrino Masses and Mixings from Warped Geometry
Updated: 2009-01-28T14:58:34Z
Published: 2008-05-30T17:26:39Z
Primary category: hep-ph
Journal reference: JHEP 01 (2009) 077
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2009/01/077
Authors: Gilad Perez; Lisa Randall

Abstract:
The paper demonstrates that flavor symmetries in warped geometry can provide a
natural explanation for large mixing angles and economically explain the
distinction between the quark and lepton flavor sectors. It shows how to
generate Majorana neutrino masses with a gauged U(1)_{B-L} symmetry broken in
the UV. The model requires lepton minimal flavor violation, in which only the
IR-brane Yukawa matrices break the flavor symmetries. With lepton MFV, a
GIM-like mechanism prevents dangerous charged-lepton flavor-changing processes
and permits charged-lepton flavor change only in the presence of the neutrino
Yukawa interaction.

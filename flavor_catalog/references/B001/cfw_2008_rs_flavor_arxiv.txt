Extracted context from the arXiv abstract:
- The paper re-examines flavor constraints in standard Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios.
- It reports that the KK gluon mass should generically be heavier than about 21 TeV in the standard RS scenario.
- It compares composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs flavor structure to RS flavor and reports bounds of at least about 33 TeV in the pseudo-Goldstone scenario under the assumptions described in the paper.
- The abstract emphasizes RS-GIM suppression but strong remaining flavor bounds, with possible dependence on gluon boundary kinetic terms.

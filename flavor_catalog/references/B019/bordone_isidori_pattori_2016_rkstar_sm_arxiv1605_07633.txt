Title: On the Standard Model predictions for R_K and R_K*
Authors: Marzia Bordone, Gino Isidori, Andrea Pattori
Year: 2016
Local snapshot purpose: SM cleanliness and QED/radiative-correction context for R_K* ratios.

Relevant arXiv-source excerpt:
The paper defines R_{K^{(*)}} as B -> K^{(*)} mu+ mu- over B -> K^{(*)} e+ e-
in specified q^2 bins and emphasizes that these ratios are clean probes of
physics beyond the SM with small theoretical uncertainties.

For central q^2, the authors quote
  R_{K*}[1.1, 6.0]^{SM} = 1.00 +/- 0.01_QED.

For low q^2, their added note gives
  R_{K*}[0.045, 1.1]^{SM} = 0.906 +/- 0.028_th,
  R_{K*}[0.1, 1.1]^{SM} = 0.983 +/- 0.014_th.

They state that a deviation of R_K or R_K* from unity exceeding the percent
level, with consistent experimental cuts and radiative treatment, is a clean
signal of physics beyond the SM.

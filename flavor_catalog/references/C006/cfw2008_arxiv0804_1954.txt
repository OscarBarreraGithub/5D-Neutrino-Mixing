Title: The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs
Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
Journal reference: JHEP 0809:008, 2008
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008
Submitted: 2008-04-14
Last revised: 2008-04-24

Extracted abstract facts:
  The paper studies flavor structure in five-dimensional warped models and
  re-examines flavor constraints on the mass scale of new physics in
  Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios.
  It finds the KK gluon mass should generically be heavier than about 21 TeV
  in standard RS-type scenarios.
  In the composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs scenario, kinetic mixing and the
  gauge-coupling-sized Yukawa structure imply a KK gluon mass of at least
  about 33 TeV.
  The abstract emphasizes that fully anarchic warped flavor is implausible
  without at least partial flavor symmetries.

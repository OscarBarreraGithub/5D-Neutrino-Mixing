Title: Search for high-mass dilepton resonances using 139 fb^{-1} of pp collision data collected at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with the ATLAS detector
Authors: ATLAS Collaboration
Published: 2019-03-14
Updated: 2019-10-21
Primary category: hep-ex
Report number: CERN-EP-2019-030
Journal reference: Phys. Lett. B 796 (2019) 68
DOI: 10.1016/j.physletb.2019.07.016

Abstract / result extract:
ATLAS searches for high-mass dielectron and dimuon resonances in the mass
range from 250 GeV to 6 TeV using Run-2 proton-proton collision data at
sqrt(s)=13 TeV, corresponding to 139 fb^{-1}. No significant deviation from
the fitted standard-model background is observed. Upper limits are placed at
95% confidence level on fiducial cross section times branching ratio for
several signal-width hypotheses.

Table 3 reports 95% CL lower limits on the mass of three Z' gauge-boson
models. For the combined dilepton channel:

  Z'_psi observed 4.5 TeV, expected 4.5 TeV.
  Z'_chi observed 4.8 TeV, expected 4.8 TeV.
  Z'_SSM observed 5.1 TeV, expected 5.1 TeV.

The paper also states that the observed fiducial cross-section times
branching-ratio limit in the combined dilepton channel ranges from 3.6 fb at
250 GeV to about 0.014 fb at 6 TeV for the zero-width signal, and from 13.1 fb
at 250 GeV to about 0.018 fb at 6 TeV for the 10% relative-width signal.

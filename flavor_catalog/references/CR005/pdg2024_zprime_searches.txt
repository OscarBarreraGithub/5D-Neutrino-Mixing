Title: Z'-Boson Searches
Authors: Particle Data Group review article
Review date in PDF: 31st May, 2024

Relevant extract:
The review summarizes searches for heavy neutral spin-1 resonances at hadron
colliders. In Section 87.4 it notes that Z' bosons with couplings to quarks
can be produced in the s-channel and would appear as resonances in the
invariant-mass distribution of their decay products. The review cites:

  [22] ATLAS, Phys. Lett. B 796, 68 (2019), arXiv:1903.06248.
  [23] CMS, JHEP 07, 208 (2021), arXiv:2103.02708.

The review states that ATLAS and CMS searches for Z' -> e+e- and Z' -> mu+mu-
have set 95% CL upper cross-section limits as low as 0.02 fb, with model
mass lower limits as high as 4.9 TeV in a single channel. It also emphasizes
that dilepton resonances determine the Z' mass and width at discovery and
that angular and rapidity distributions can help distinguish couplings to
up-type and down-type quarks.

This CR005 draft uses the PDG review as the one-place summary that the ATLAS
2019 and CMS 2021 full Run-2 dilepton searches are the relevant canonical
prompt-dilepton Z' inputs.

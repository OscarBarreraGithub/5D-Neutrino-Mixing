Title: Search for resonant and nonresonant new phenomena in high-mass dilepton final states at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
Authors: CMS Collaboration
Published: 2021-03-03
Updated: 2021-08-03

Report number: CMS-EXO-19-019, CERN-EP-2021-026
Journal reference: JHEP 07 (2021) 208
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP07(2021)208

Abstract / result extract:
A search is presented for physics beyond the standard model using electron
or muon pairs with high invariant mass. The analysis uses proton-proton
collisions collected by CMS at sqrt(s)=13 TeV during 2016-2018, with a total
integrated luminosity up to 140 fb^{-1}. No significant deviation from the
standard-model background expectation is observed.

The paper gives upper limits on the ratio of sigma(pp -> Z' -> dileptons) to
sigma(pp -> Z -> dileptons), and interprets them as lower mass limits for
spin-1 resonances. Table 5 reports observed and expected 95% CL lower limits
on the masses of Z'_SSM and Z'_psi. For the combined ee + mumu channel:

  Z'_SSM observed 5.15 TeV, expected 5.14 TeV.
  Z'_psi observed 4.56 TeV, expected 4.55 TeV.

The table assumes signal widths of 3.0% of the resonance mass for Z'_SSM and
0.6% for Z'_psi. This CR005 draft uses the Z'_SSM combined result as the
canonical current high-mass dilepton spin-1 benchmark limit.

Title: Search for the Rare Decay KL -> pi0 e+ e-
Collaboration: KTeV

Relevant abstract and introduction extraction:
The KTeV/E799 experiment at Fermilab searched for KL -> pi0 e+ e-. This mode
is expected to have a significant CP-violating component. For the 1999-2000
data set, one event was observed with an expected background of 0.99 +/- 0.35
events. KTeV set a 90% CL limit BR(KL -> pi0 e+ e-) < 3.5 x 10^-10. Combining
with the 1997 KTeV result yielded the final KTeV limit:
BR(KL -> pi0 e+ e-) < 2.8 x 10^-10 at 90% CL.

The paper states that in the Standard Model there are direct and indirect CPV
contributions to the amplitude plus an interference term. The indirect
component is known from KS -> pi0 e+ e- and appears to dominate. The direct
component was estimated to be about 3 to 6 x 10^-12, and the two CPV
contributions together give BR(KL -> pi0 e+ e-)_CPV in the range
8 to 45 x 10^-12. The CP-conserving amplitude proceeds through
pi0 gamma* gamma* states and can be determined from KL -> pi0 gamma gamma.
The paper quotes a Standard-Model branching ratio around 3 x 10^-11, dominated
by CPV, with a 40% contribution from direct CPV through the interference term.

Title: First exploratory calculation of the long-distance contributions to the
rare kaon decays K -> pi l+ l-
Authors: Norman H. Christ, Xu Feng, Andreas Juettner, Andrew Lawson, Antonin
Portelli, Christopher T. Sachrajda

Relevant abstract extraction:
Rare K -> pi l+ l- decays proceed through a flavor-changing neutral current and
are naturally suppressed, making them sensitive to possible new physics. The
CP-conserving channels are dominated by single-photon exchange and contain a
sizeable long-distance hadronic contribution, described as the current major
source of theoretical uncertainty. The paper outlines a lattice-QCD method for
computing long-distance rare-kaon amplitudes and presents exploratory RBC/UKQCD
domain-wall-fermion results. The ensembles have M_pi approximately 430 MeV and
M_K approximately 625 MeV; for K+ -> pi+ l+ l- the quoted exploratory form
factor values are V(z)=1.37(36), 0.68(39), 0.96(64) at three negative z values.

Use in K008 catalog:
This is a post-2008 development showing movement toward first-principles
long-distance control for K -> pi l+ l- amplitudes. It is not a replacement for
the KTeV/PDG experimental K008 limit.

Source: Monika Blanke, Andrzej J. Buras, Bjoern Duling, Katrin Gemmler,
and Stefania Gori, "Rare K and B Decays in a Warped Extra Dimension with
Custodial Protection"
arXiv:0812.3803v3
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2009/03/108
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/0812.3803
Access date: 2026-05-17

ArXiv abstract-page snapshot for T014 post-2008 RS/Z-FCNC relevance.

The paper presents a complete study of rare K and B meson decays in a warped
extra-dimensional model with custodial protection of both diagonal and
non-diagonal Z d_L^i dbar_L^j couplings. It includes K+ -> pi+ nu nubar,
K_L -> pi0 nu nubar, K_L -> pi0 l+ l-, K_L -> mu+ mu-, B_{s,d} -> mu+ mu-,
B -> K nu nubar, B -> K* nu nubar, and B -> X_{s,d} nu nubar.

In addition to SM one-loop contributions these processes receive tree-level
contributions from the Z boson and the new heavy electroweak gauge bosons.
The authors find contributions dominated by tree-level Z exchanges governed
by right-handed couplings to down-type quarks. After imposing Delta F=2
constraints and fitting quark masses and CKM parameters, rare K branching
ratios can differ significantly from SM predictions, while B-decay effects
are more modest because custodial protection is more effective in B decays
than in K decays. The paper also discusses the absence of custodial protection
of Z d_L^i dbar_L^j couplings, where rare-B modifications can be spectacular.

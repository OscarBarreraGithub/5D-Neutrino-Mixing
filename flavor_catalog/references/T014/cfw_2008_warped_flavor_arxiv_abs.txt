"The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs"
arXiv:0804.1954v2
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/0804.1954
Access date: 2026-05-17

ArXiv abstract-page snapshot for T014 paper-era baseline.

The authors study the flavor structure of 5D warped models that provide a
dual description of a composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs. They re-examine flavor
constraints in standard Randall-Sundrum scenarios and find that the KK gluon
mass should generically be heavier than about 21 TeV. They compare composite
Higgs models to RS, finding new contributions to flavor violation still
suppressed by the RS-GIM mechanism but enhancing flavor-violation amplitudes.
In the pseudo-Goldstone scenario, the Yukawa couplings being set by a gauge
coupling implies a KK gluon mass of at least about 33 TeV. The abstract states
that these strong bounds make the fully anarchic approach to flavor in warped
extra dimensions implausible without partial flavor symmetries.

This source is used as paper-era warped-flavor context only; the 21 TeV and
33 TeV numbers are theory normalization/reference scales and are not entered
as T014 pdg_or_equivalent measured observables.

Abstract extract:
The authors derive bounds on flavor-violating couplings of the Z boson to
quarks and present future sensitivity projections. The abstract states that
current bounds on FV couplings are O(10^-9) for Z couplings to cu and sd,
O(10^-7) for bd, O(10^-6) for bs, and O(10^-3) for tu and tc. It also states
that low-energy flavor experiments provide significantly stronger constraints
than current collider searches.

Direct-search section extract:
The simplest bounds can be extracted from direct searches of hadronic
Z -> q_i qbar_j decays with i != j. At tree level,

  Gamma(Z -> q_i qbar_j) = M_Z/(12 pi) (|g_L^ij|^2 + |g_R^ij|^2),

neglecting final-state quark masses. Bounds on non-standard hadronic Z decays
are obtained from the disagreement between SM predictions and the Z hadronic
decay width. These bounds are given as

  Br(Z -> q_i qbar_j) < 2.9 x 10^-3

which translates to

  sqrt(|g_L^ij|^2 + |g_R^ij|^2) < 4.96 x 10^-2,

where {i,j} includes cu, bs, bd, cu conjugate, bs conjugate, bd conjugate,
and sd. The text notes that the top quark is not included because the Z does
not decay to an on-shell top quark.

Table I / meson-oscillation extract:

Direct Searches, bs: sqrt(|g_L^bs|^2 + |g_R^bs|^2) < 4.96 x 10^-2
Direct Searches, bd: sqrt(|g_L^bd|^2 + |g_R^bd|^2) < 4.96 x 10^-2
Direct Searches, sd: sqrt(|g_L^sd|^2 + |g_R^sd|^2) < 4.96 x 10^-2

B^0 - Bbar^0 oscillation:
  sqrt(|g_L^bd|^2 + |g_R^bd|^2) < 1.11 x 10^-7

B_s - Bbar_s oscillation:
  sqrt(|g_L^bs|^2 + |g_R^bs|^2) < 5.32 x 10^-6

K^0 - Kbar^0 oscillation:
  sqrt(|g_L^sd|^2 + |g_R^sd|^2) in [-4.64, 4.64] x 10^-9

The authors state that they shift bounds to 90% CL for consistency in their
coupling-bound table; the underlying branching-ratio direct-search bound above
is quoted as the 2.9 x 10^-3 non-standard hadronic-Z limit.

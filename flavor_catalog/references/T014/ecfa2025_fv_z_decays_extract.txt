Section 5.4.2, "Flavour changing Z-boson decays":

The SM predicts the branching ratio of Z -> bs, bd, cu to be strongly
suppressed by the GIM mechanism, while the lepton flavour violating processes,
Z -> e mu, e tau, mu tau, are forbidden. Precise measurements of these decay
channels are then highly sensitive to possible New Physics contributions. The
report parametrises the latter in terms of effective FCNC couplings to the Z
boson,

  L contains g_L^ij (q_i,L gamma_mu q_j,L) Z^mu
             + g_R^ij (q_i,R gamma_mu q_j,R) Z^mu,

where ij = bs, bd, cu.

Updated computations yield:

  B(Z -> bs) = (4.2 +/- 0.7) x 10^-8
  B(Z -> bd) = (1.8 +/- 0.3) x 10^-9
  B(Z -> cu) = (1.4 +/- 0.2) x 10^-18

with B(Z -> q_i q_j) defined as the sum of particle and antiparticle final
states. The reported uncertainties contain both numerical-input theory
uncertainties and leading two-loop corrections to the amplitudes.

Existing direct limits on non-standard hadronic decays of the Z follow from
the agreement of the measurement and the SM prediction for the Z hadronic
width, giving:

  B(Z -> qq') < 2.9 x 10^-3 at 95% CL.

The report states that low-energy constraints are much more powerful. For
Z -> bd and Z -> bs, flavour-changing processes essentially constrain the
new-physics effect below the SM value, so the direct-observation target is
effectively the SM value within uncertainties. For Z -> uc the report quotes
B(Z -> uc) < 4 x 10^-7 from a flavour global fit.

FCC-ee projection excerpt:
For 0.1% systematic uncertainties the expected reaches are

  B(Z -> bs) < 7.33 x 10^-6
  B(Z -> cu) < 4.06 x 10^-4

and using only a b-tagger gives the weaker projected constraint

  B(Z -> bd) < 2.45 x 10^-4.

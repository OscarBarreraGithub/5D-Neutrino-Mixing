Minimal extract from "11. Status of Higgs Boson Physics", section 11.4.3.2
"Flavor violating decays" (web/PDF text extraction checked 2026-05-16; no
publisher PDF saved):

The review states that the lepton-flavor-violating decays h -> e mu,
h -> e tau, and h -> mu tau are forbidden in the Standard Model but can arise
in BSM scenarios, including 2HDM and composite-Higgs models. These decays probe
off-diagonal Yukawa couplings Y_e_mu, Y_e_tau, and Y_mu_tau.

The full 13 TeV Run-2 ATLAS and CMS datasets set 95% CL upper limits on LFV
Higgs branching ratios. For h -> e mu, the observed (expected) limits are:
  B(h -> e mu) < 6.2 x 10^-5 (5.9 x 10^-5) from ATLAS [273].
  B(h -> e mu) < 4.4 x 10^-5 (4.7 x 10^-5) from CMS [441].

The same paragraph notes that these numbers correspond to exotic decays of a
125 GeV Higgs boson. It also notes that the CMS e mu search was expanded to
the mass range 110-160 GeV and reported a mild excess near 146 GeV with global
significance 2.8 standard deviations, insufficient for a conclusion.

The review further states that the most stringent constraint on h -> e mu is
indirect and comes from mu -> e gamma searches; this catalog entry treats that
as auxiliary context, not as the direct measured h -> e mu branching-fraction
observable.

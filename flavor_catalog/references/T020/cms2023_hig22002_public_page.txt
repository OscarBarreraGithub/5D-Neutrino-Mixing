Title:
Search for the lepton-flavor violating decay of the Higgs boson and additional
Higgs bosons in the e mu final state in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) =
13 TeV

Authors: CMS Collaboration
Public result: CMS-HIG-22-002
arXiv: 2305.18106v2
Journal reference: Phys. Rev. D 108, 072004 (2023)
DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.108.072004

Relevant public-page summary:

The CMS search uses 138 fb^-1 of pp collision data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV and
looks for the lepton-flavor-violating decay of the 125 GeV Higgs boson and for
additional Higgs bosons X with masses in the 110-160 GeV range, in the e mu
final state. No excess is observed for the 125 GeV Higgs boson.

The observed (expected) upper limit on the branching fraction is:
  B(H -> e mu) < 4.4 (4.7) x 10^-5 at 95% CL.

The public summary calls this the most stringent direct limit set thus far.
For the wider X -> e mu mass scan, the largest excess is around 146 GeV with
local (global) significance 3.8 (2.8) standard deviations; the summary states
that this is insufficient to draw conclusions.

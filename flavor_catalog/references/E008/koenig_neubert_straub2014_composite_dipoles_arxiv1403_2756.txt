Title: Dipole operator constraints on composite Higgs models
Authors: Matthias Koenig, Matthias Neubert, David M. Straub
arXiv:1403.2756
Published DOI: 10.1140/epjc/s10052-014-2945-9

Relevant arXiv abstract extract:
  Flavour- and CP-violating electromagnetic or chromomagnetic dipole
  operators in the quark sector are generated in many new-physics models and
  are strongly constrained by neutron EDM measurements and flavor-changing
  observables. The models studied can be viewed as low-energy limits of
  composite Higgs or warped extra-dimensional models. With wrong-chirality
  Yukawa couplings, the paper finds a strong neutron-EDM bound independent
  of the flavor structure.

Relevant extracted text:
  The quark EDMs and CEDMs are related to flavor-conserving Wilson
  coefficients by
    d_q = e m_q Im C_qqgamma(mu_l) / (8 pi^2),
    tilde d_q = g_s m_q Im C_qqg(mu_l) / (8 pi^2),
  at a hadronic scale mu_l of order 1 GeV.
  The paper uses the QCD-sum-rule neutron relation
    d_n = (1 +/- 0.5) [1.4(d_d - 1/4 d_u)
                       + 1.1 e(tilde d_d + 1/2 tilde d_u)].
  The paper's then-current neutron limit was
    |d_n| < 2.9 x 10^-26 e cm at 90% C.L.
  Its model-independent table gives one-at-a-time imaginary-coefficient
  scale bounds at 1 TeV, including Q_uug: 1.45 TeV and Q_ddg: 3.79 TeV,
  using that older neutron limit.

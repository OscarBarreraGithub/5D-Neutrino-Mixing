Title: Search for vector-like T and B quark pairs in final states with leptons at $\sqrt{s} =$ 13 TeV
Authors: CMS Collaboration
Published: 2018-05-12T18:29:09Z
Updated: 2018-09-05T06:29:33Z
Primary category: hep-ex
Journal reference: JHEP 08 (2018) 177
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP08(2018)177

Abstract:
A search is presented for pair production of heavy vector-like T and B quarks in proton-
proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} =$ 13 TeV. The data sample corresponds to an integrated
luminosity of 35.9 fb$^{-1}$, collected with the CMS detector at the CERN LHC in 2016.
Pair production of T quarks would result in a wide range of final states, since vector-
like T quarks of charge 2$e$/3 are predicted to decay to bW, tZ, and tH. Likewise,
vector-like B quarks are predicted to decay to tW, bZ, and bH. Three channels are
considered, corresponding to final states with a single lepton, two leptons with the
same sign of the electric charge, or at least three leptons. The results exclude T
quarks with masses below 1140-1300 GeV and B quarks with masses below 910-1240 GeV for
various branching fraction combinations, extending the reach of previous CMS searches by
200-600 GeV.

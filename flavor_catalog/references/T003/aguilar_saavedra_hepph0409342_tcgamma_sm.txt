Title:
Top flavour-changing neutral interactions: theoretical expectations and
experimental detection

Author: J. A. Aguilar-Saavedra

Relevant TeX-source excerpts, normalized:

The effective FCNC Lagrangian includes a photon dipole term with coefficient
lambda_qt:
  e lambda_qt qbar (lambda_v + lambda_a gamma_5)
    i sigma_{mu nu} q^nu / m_t t A^mu.

The corresponding branching-ratio normalization is
  Br(t -> q gamma) = 0.428 lambda_qt^2.

The paper gives the Standard Model prediction
  Br(t -> c gamma) =
  (4.6^{+1.2}_{-1.0} +/- 0.2 +/- 0.4^{+1.6}_{-0.5}) x 10^-14.

Table 1 lists the central SM entry for t -> c gamma as 4.6 x 10^-14 and
representative extended-model entries including 7.5 x 10^-9 for up-type quark
singlets, approximately 10^-6 for a general 2HDM, approximately 10^-9 for a
flavour-conserving 2HDM, 2 x 10^-6 for the MSSM, and 1 x 10^-6 for
R-parity-violating SUSY.

These are theory-context values, not measured observables.

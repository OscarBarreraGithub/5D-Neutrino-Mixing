Title:
The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs

Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
Submitted: 2008-04-12; last revised: 2008-07-09, version v2
Journal reference: JHEP 0809 (2008) 008
Related DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.0804.1954

Relevant source summary:

The paper re-examines flavor constraints in standard Randall-Sundrum-type
scenarios. It explains that non-universal fermion profiles and rotations to
the mass basis generate flavor off-diagonal couplings to heavy gauge bosons,
with RS-GIM suppression. The abstract states that ordinary RS flavor
constraints generically push the KK-gluon mass scale to about 21 TeV and that
the composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs setup gives a corresponding scale of
about 33 TeV.

The T003 catalog entry uses this as the 2008 RS-flavor baseline. Any top
photon FCNC limit is post-2008 collider information layered on top of this
baseline, not a measured observable in the CFW paper.

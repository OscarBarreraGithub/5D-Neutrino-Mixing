Title:
Top flavour-changing neutral interactions: theoretical expectations and
experimental detection

Author: J. A. Aguilar-Saavedra
Submitted: 2004-09-29; last revised: 2005-07-06, version v4
Journal reference: Acta Phys. Polon. B35 (2004) 2695-2710
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.hep-ph/0409342

Relevant abstract excerpt:

Top flavour-changing neutral interactions with a light quark q = u,c and a
gauge or Higgs boson are very suppressed within the Standard Model, but can
reach observable levels in many of its extensions. The review discusses
effective Ztq, gamma tq, gtq, and Htq vertices and their LHC and future
linear-collider detection channels.

Relevant TeX-source excerpt:

The review gives Br(t -> c Z) approximately 1 x 10^-14 and states that
decays t -> u Z, t -> u gamma, t -> u g, and t -> u H are smaller than the
corresponding charm modes by |V_ub / V_cb|^2 approximately 0.0079.

In its model-comparison table, the Standard Model entry for t -> u Z is
8 x 10^-17. The same table lists new-physics examples with much larger
rates, e.g. models with extra quarks at 1.1 x 10^-4, MSSM examples around
2 x 10^-6, and R-parity-violating SUSY around 3 x 10^-5.

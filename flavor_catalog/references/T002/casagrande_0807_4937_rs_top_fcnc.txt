Title:
Flavor Physics in the Randall-Sundrum Model: I. Theoretical Setup and
Electroweak Precision Tests

Authors: S. Casagrande, F. Goertz, U. Haisch, M. Neubert, T. Pfoh
Submitted: 2008-07-31; last revised: 2008-10-20, version v3
Report number: MZ-TH/08-18
Journal reference: JHEP 0810 (2008) 094
Related DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/10/094
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.0807.4937

Relevant abstract excerpt:

The paper presents a complete discussion of tree-level flavor-changing
effects in the RS model with a brane-localized Higgs sector and bulk gauge
and matter fields. It solves the bulk gauge and fermion equations with
general generation mixing, studies flavor-changing couplings of the SM W and
Z bosons and their KK excitations, and includes rare decays
t -> c(u) Z and t -> c(u) h.

Relevant TeX-source excerpts:

The authors state that flavor-changing top-quark couplings are interesting in
RS because the top quark is closest to the IR brane and couples most strongly
to KK gauge-boson excitations, while up-sector flavor transitions are less
constrained by kaon and B physics than down-sector transitions.

The t -> u Z branching ratio and couplings are obtained from the t -> c Z
expressions by replacing flavor indices 23 with 13 and m_c with m_u. The
authors note that RS-GIM strongly suppresses t -> u Z relative to t -> c Z.
They state that t -> u Z is typically suppressed by two orders of magnitude
relative to t -> c Z, mainly from |F(c_Q1)| / |F(c_Q2)| ~ lambda and the
small up-quark mass, and that in the minimal RS model this makes LHC testing
of the t -> u Z prediction very difficult.

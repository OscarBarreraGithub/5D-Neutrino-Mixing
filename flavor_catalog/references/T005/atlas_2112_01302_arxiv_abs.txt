Title:
Search for flavour-changing neutral-current interactions of a top quark and a
gluon in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with the ATLAS detector

Authors: ATLAS Collaboration
Submitted: 2021-12-02; last revised: 2022-07-01, version v2
Journal reference: Eur. Phys. J. C 82 (2022) 334
Related DOI: 10.1140/epjc/s10052-022-10182-7
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2112.01302

Relevant arXiv abstract values:

The analysis searches for production of a single top quark through
left-handed FCNC interactions of a top quark, a gluon, and an up or charm
quark. The considered processes are u + g -> t and c + g -> t.

The data were collected by ATLAS in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV with
integrated luminosity 139 fb^{-1}. Events with exactly one electron or muon,
one b-tagged jet, and missing transverse momentum are selected.

No excess over the background-only hypothesis is found. The observed 95% CL
limits are:
- sigma(u+g -> t) * B(t -> Wb) * B(W -> l nu) < 3.0 pb.
- sigma(c+g -> t) * B(t -> Wb) * B(W -> l nu) < 4.7 pb.

The arXiv abstract states that B(W -> l nu)=0.325 is the sum over the three
leptonic W modes used in the interpretation.

EFT interpretation:
- |C_uG^{ut}| / Lambda^2 < 0.057 TeV^{-2}.
- |C_uG^{ct}| / Lambda^2 < 0.14 TeV^{-2}.

Equivalent FCNC top-decay branching-fraction limits:
- B(t -> u + g) < 0.61e-4.
- B(t -> c + g) < 3.7e-4.

Title:
The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs

Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
Submitted: 2008-04-12; last revised: 2008-07-09, version v2
Journal reference: JHEP 0809 (2008) 008
Related DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008
arXiv DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.0804.1954

Relevant TeX-source excerpts:

The abstract says the authors re-examine flavor constraints in standard
Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios and find that the KK gluon mass should
generically be heavier than about 21 TeV. In their composite
pseudo-Goldstone Higgs setup they find flavor-violating effects that make
the corresponding KK-gluon scale about 33 TeV.

The paper explains that the largest FCNC contribution in the standard RS
setup is generated by exchange of heavy gauge bosons, especially the lightest
KK gluon. The mass-basis off-diagonal KK-gluon couplings are controlled by
fermion profiles and exhibit RS-GIM suppression.

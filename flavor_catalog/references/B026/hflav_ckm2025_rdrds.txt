<h2>Average of R(D) and R(D<sup>*</sup>) for CKM 2025</h2>

<p> Last update on 28.09.2025

<p>This web page contains the averages of the ratios of Branching Fractions 
R(D*)=BF(B&rarr;D*&tau;&nu;<sub>&tau;</sub>)/BF(B&rarr;D* l &nu;<sub>l</sub>) 
and R(D)=BF(B&rarr;D&tau;&nu;<sub>&tau;</sub>)/BF(B&rarr;D l &nu;<sub>l</sub>). 
<br> In the present average we consider the following measurements:</p>
<ul>
    <li>BaBar Collaboration: Phys.Rev.Lett 109, 101802 (2012) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1205.5442">[arXiv:1205.5442]</a> and 
    Phys.Rev.D 88, 072012 (2013) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1303.0571">[arXiv:1303.0571]</a>    
    <br>Combined measurement of R(D) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>) based on the hadronic tagging sample and it is a combined. 
    We use the results obtained imposing the isospin relations R(D<sup>0</sup>)=R(D<sup>+</sup>) and R(D<sup>*0</sup>)=R(D<sup>*+</sup>).  
    </li> 

    <li>Belle Collaboration: Phys.Rev. D92, 072014 (2015) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1507.03233">[arXiv:1507.03233]</a>
    <br>Similar to the BaBar analysis: combined  measurement of R(D) and R(D*), based on the hadronic tagging sample.
    </li>

    <li>Belle Collaboration: Phys.Rev.Lett. 118, 211801 (2017) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1612.00529">[arXiv:1612.00529]</a> and
    Phys.Rev.D 97, 012004 (2018) <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.00129">[arXiv:1709.00129]</a>    
    <br>Measurement of R(D<sup>*+</sup>) based on an hadronic tagging sample. The &tau; is reconstructed in the hadronic
    decay channels &tau;&rarr;&pi;&nu; and &tau;&rarr;&rho;&nu;. This is a simultaneous measurement of R(D*) and the 
    &tau; polarization.
    </li>

    <li>Belle Collaboration: Phys.Rev.Lett. 124 (2020) 16, 161803 <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.05864">[arXiv:1910.05864]</a>
    <br>Combined measurement of R(D) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>), based on a semileptonic tagging method. This supersedes the published  
    measurement: Phys.Rev. D94, 072007 (2016) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1607.07923">[arXiv:1607.07923]</a>
 
    <li>LHCb Collaboration: Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 111802 <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.02886">[arXiv:2302.02886]</a> 
    <br>Combined measurement of R(D) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>). Only the &tau;&rarr;&mu;&nu;<sub>&mu;</sub>&nu;<sub>&tau;</sub> decay mode is used. 
    </li>

    <li>LHCb Collaboration: Phys. Rev. D 108, 012018 <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.01463">[arXiv:2305.01463]</a> and 
    <br>Measurement of R(D<sup>*+</sup>) where &tau; is reconstructed in the three-prong hadronic decay channels &tau;&rarr;3&pi;(&pi;<sup>0</sup>)&nu;.

     <li>LHCb Collaboration: Phys. Rev. Lett. 134, 061801 (2025) <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.03387">[arXiv:2406.03387]</a>
    <br>Measurement of R(D<sup>+</sup>) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>), using the &tau;&rarr;&mu;&nu;<sub>&mu;</sub>&nu;<sub>&tau;</sub> decay mode.
     </li>

     <li>Belle II Collaboration: Phys. Rev. D 112 (2025) 3, 032010 <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.11220">[arXiv:2504.11220]</a>
    <br>Measurement of R(D<sup>+</sup>) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>), using semileptonic B tagging decay.
     </li>

     <li>Belle II Collaboration: Presented at CKM 2025 <a href="https://indico.cern.ch/event/1440982/contributions/6576608/attachments/3136874/5566279/2025_CKM_LFU_belleII_itsaklid.pdf">[talk]</a>
	 <br>Measurement of R(D<sup>+</sup>) and R(D<sup>*+</sup>), using hadronic B tagging decay, supersedes the Belle II Measurement of R(D*), based on the hadronic tagging sample Phys. Rev. D 110, 072020 (2024) <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.02840">[arXiv:2401.02840]</a>.
     </li>
    </li>
 
</ul>    

</p>

<br>In the average we assume 100% correlation for the systematic uncertainties 
associated with the B&rarr;D<sup>(*)</sup> form factors, the D<sup>**</sup> composition and shapes, and the &tau; branching fractions. 
The other uncertainties are considered uncorrelated between the various experiments. The average is obtained using the the <a href="https://gitlab.cern.ch/vchobano/combination-code">CoCo</a> averaging tool.

<h3> Compatibility with SM predictions </h3>
<p> SM predictions for R(D) based on following Lattice calculations:</p>
<ul>
    <li> R(D) =0.299 &plusmn; 0.011, J.A.Bailey et al. [FNAL/MILC Collaboration], Phys.Rev.D 92, 034506 (2015)<a 
href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1503.07237">arXiv:1503.07237 [hep-lat]</a></li>
    <li> R(D) =0.300 &plusmn; 0.008, H. Na et al., Phys.Rev.D 92, 054410 (2015) <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1505.03925">arXiv:1505.03925 [hep-lat]</a></li>
</ul>

The prediction obtained combining the two calculation above FLAG Collaboration  <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.04268">arXiv:2411.04268 [hep-lat] </a>) is R(D) =0.2938 &plusmn;  0.054. 

</p> P. Gambino and D. Bigi in  Phys.Rev.D 94, 094008 (2016) <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.08030">arXiv:1606.08030 [hep-ph]</a></li> 
combined the two lattice calculations, with the experimental Form Factor of B&rarr; D l &nu; from
BaBar (2010) and Belle (2016), obtaining R(D) = 0.299 &plusmn; 0.003. The result is compatible with the above calculations, 
but more accurate. Similar calculations were performed more recently by other groups (see table below for references).

<br>
<p> The SM prediction for R(D<sup>*</sup>) widely used for many  HFLAV reports was: </p>
<ul>
  <li> R(D<sup>*</sup>)=0.252 &plusmn;  0.003, S.Fajfer, J.F.Kamenik, and I.Nisandzic, Phys.Rev.D85(2012) 094025 <a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1203.2654">arXiv:1203.2654 [hep-ph]</a> </li>
</ul>

<p>
In 2021 FNAL/MILC released the first unquenched Lattice calculation of B&rarr;D<sup>*</sup>l &nu; form factors at non-zero recoil,
A.Bazavov et al. [<a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/2105.14019">Eur. Phys. J. C 82, 1141 (2022)</a>] </td>, 
predicting a value of R(D<sup>*</sup>)=0.265 &plusmn;  0.013, using only Lattice inputs. 
In addition, since 2023, two indipendent lattice calculations at non-zero recoil are available: from HPQCD <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03137">arXiv:2304.03137[hep-lat]</a> (R(D<sup>*</sup>)=0.273 &plusmn;  0.015), and JLQCD <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.05657">arXiv:2306.05657[hep-lat]</a> (R(D<sup>*</sup>)=0.252 &plusmn;  0.022), have been released. 

Various indipendent analyses of these three lattice data has been performed by various groups. The first were performed by G. Martinelli et al. [<a href="http://arxiv.org/abs/2109.15248">Eur. Phys. J. C 84 (2024) 400</a>] and I.Ray et al. [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.11855">JHEP 01 (2024) 022</a>], and the results reported in the table below. In these papers result combined with experimental inputs from Belle and Belle II on B&rarr;D<sup>*</sup>l &nu; are reported along with results based on lattice only calculations.

Title: The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs
Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
Published: 2008
Journal reference: JHEP 0809:008, 2008
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008

Relevant abstract text:
The authors study the flavor structure of 5D warped models and re-examine the
flavor constraints on the mass scale of new physics in standard
Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios. They find that the KK gluon mass should
generically be heavier than about 21 TeV. In composite Higgs variants,
additional flavor-violating contributions and gauge-size Yukawa couplings imply
the KK gluon mass should be at least about 33 TeV. The abstract frames fully
anarchic warped flavor as strongly constrained and motivates partial flavor
symmetries.

Title: Neutrino Tridents at DUNE

  Alexandre Sousa, Michael Wallbank
Publication: Phys. Rev. D 100, 115029 (2019)

Minimal extracted facts:
- The paper gives the historical muonic-trident ratios:
  CHARM-II: sigma_exp/sigma_SM = 1.58 +/- 0.64;
  CCFR: sigma_exp/sigma_SM = 0.82 +/- 0.28;
  NuTeV: sigma_exp/sigma_SM = 0.72^{+1.73}_{-0.72}.
- The text says CHARM-II and CCFR found rates compatible with Standard Model
  expectations, while no signal could be established at NuTeV.
- The DUNE arXiv abstract says the near detector could measure
  nu_mu -> nu_mu mu+ mu- with about 25% accuracy after about 3 years each in
  neutrino and antineutrino modes.
- The v2 TeX body/conclusion uses a 40% baseline accuracy and discusses a
  25% contour as an improved scenario.
- The paper quotes roughly 240 nu_mu -> nu_mu mu+ mu- trident events per year
  at the DUNE near detector before analysis cuts, and O(10) signal events/year
  after the detailed selection.

Abstract/value extract used in B004:
The decay-time-dependent CP asymmetry in Bs0 -> J/psi(mu+mu-) K+K- decays is
measured using proton-proton collision data corresponding to 6 fb^-1 at 13 TeV.
The sample contains approximately 349000 Bs0 signal decays with K+K- mass near
the phi(1020) resonance.

The values obtained are:
  phi_s = -0.039 +/- 0.022 +/- 0.006 rad,
  DeltaGamma_s = 0.0845 +/- 0.0044 +/- 0.0024 ps^-1,
  Gamma_s - Gamma_d = -0.0056^{+0.0013}_{-0.0015} +/- 0.0014 ps^-1,
where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second systematic.
The abstract states that these were the most precise single measurements to
date and were consistent with Standard Model expectations.

Introductory SM comparison extract:
Neglecting subleading loop contributions in b -> c cbar s transitions,
phi_s is predicted in the Standard Model to equal -2 beta_s. Global CKM fits
quoted by the paper give
  -2 beta_s = -0.0368^{+0.0009}_{-0.0006} rad.
At the time of the paper, the world average quoted in the introduction was
  phi_s^ccs = -0.049 +/- 0.019 rad.

Conclusion/combination extract:
The paper reports that its result supersedes the previous Run 2 LHCb
measurement in the same decay. Its combination with previous LHCb measurements
in Bs0 -> J/psi K+K- gives
  phi_s = -0.044 +/- 0.020 rad and |lambda| = 0.990 +/- 0.010.
The combination of all LHCb phi_s measurements in b -> c cbar s Bs0 decays
gives
  phi_s = -0.031 +/- 0.018 rad.
The paper also reports no evidence for polarization dependence of phi_s.

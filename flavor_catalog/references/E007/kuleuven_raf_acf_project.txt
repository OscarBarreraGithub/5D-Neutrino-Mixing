                    <h2>Project summary</h2>
                    <div class="text-justify">
                        <p>In recent decades, several experiments have been conducted to find the presence of charge-parity&nbsp;(CP) symmetry violation in strong interactions. The presence of an electric dipole moment (EDM) in quantum systems would be a direct indication of such a&nbsp;symmetry breaking. In atoms, an EDM can be produced by a nuclear Schiff moment. However, such an EDM would be small, making it difficult to measure.&nbsp;According to theoretical predictions,&nbsp;the Schiff moment is amplified in octupole shaped&nbsp;nuclei. However, most of these types of atomic nuclei are radioactive (such as neutron-rich Ra and&nbsp;Ac-isotopes). By embedding these isotopes in a dipolar molecule, the sensitivity to their Schiff moment can be enhanced by several orders of magnitude. Consequently, radioactive molecules such as RaF&nbsp;and AcF are&nbsp;predicted to be the most sensitive probes to search for the existence of an EDM. However, in order&nbsp;to detect such an EDM, the molecular structure of&nbsp;these molecules must be&nbsp;well known. Thus, the current doctoral proposal is&nbsp;to study the first molecular structure of AcF using collinear resonance ionization spectroscopy (CRIS) at CERN, as well as to unravel the first hyperfine spectroscopy of RaF&nbsp;molecules. The latter builds on the first spectroscopy studies of RaF,&nbsp;which were realized by our research group a few years ago.</p>

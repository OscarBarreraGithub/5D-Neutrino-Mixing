Title: Search for W' bosons decaying to a top and a bottom quark in leptonic final states in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
Authors: CMS Collaboration
Journal reference: JHEP 05 (2024) 046
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP05(2024)046
Report number: CMS-B2G-20-012, CERN-EP-2023-213

Abstract/result extract:
A search for W' bosons decaying to a top and a bottom quark in final
states including an electron or a muon is performed with the CMS detector
at the LHC. The analyzed data correspond to 138 fb^{-1} of proton-proton
collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV. Good agreement with the Standard Model
expectation is observed and no evidence for a W' boson is found over the
mass range examined. The largest observed deviation is for m_W' = 3.8 TeV
with a relative decay width of 1%, with local and global significances of
2.6 and 2.0 standard deviations. Upper limits on production cross sections
of W' bosons decaying to top and bottom quarks are set. Left- and
right-handed W' bosons with m_W' below 3.9 and 4.3 TeV, respectively, are
excluded at the 95% confidence level under the narrow-width assumption.
Limits are also set for relative decay widths up to 30%.

Title: LHC Signals for Warped Electroweak Charged Gauge Bosons
Authors: Kaustubh Agashe, Shrihari Gopalakrishna, Tao Han, Gui-Yu Huang, Amarjit Soni
Journal reference: Phys. Rev. D 80, 075007 (2009)
DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.80.075007

Abstract/theory extract:
This paper studies LHC signals for Kaluza-Klein excitations of
electroweak charged gauge bosons in a warped extra dimension with Standard
Model fields in the bulk. The scenario addresses both the Planck-weak and
flavor hierarchy problems. There are two charged states with suppressed
couplings to light quarks and leptons and enhanced couplings to top and
bottom quarks. Unlike the neutral electroweak KK gauge bosons, the
top-plus-bottom final state has no irreducible QCD background from a
possibly degenerate KK gluon, making it useful for discovery. Decays to
longitudinal W, Z, and Higgs bosons are also enhanced. The authors show
that such final states can give sensitivity to approximately 2 TeV
(3 TeV) masses with 100 fb^{-1} (300 fb^{-1}) and emphasize that boosted
W/top tagging improvements enhance the reach.

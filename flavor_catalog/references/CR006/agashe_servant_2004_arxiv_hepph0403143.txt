Title: Warped Unification, Proton Stability and Dark Matter
Authors: Kaustubh Agashe and Geraldine Servant
Journal reference: Phys. Rev. Lett. 93, 231805 (2004)
DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.93.231805

Abstract/theory extract:
Many extensions of the Standard Model face the problem of unsuppressed
baryon-number-violating interactions. The paper shows that solving the
baryon-number problem in non-supersymmetric grand unified theories in a
warped higher-dimensional spacetime can lead to a stable Kaluza-Klein
particle. The exotic state has the gauge quantum numbers of a right-handed
neutrino, carries fractional baryon number, and is related to the top quark
inside the higher-dimensional GUT. A combination of baryon number and
SU(3) color ensures its stability. Its relic density can be viable for
masses in the 10 GeV to few-TeV range, and other exotic GUT partners of
the top quark can be light enough for high-energy collider production with
distinctive signatures.

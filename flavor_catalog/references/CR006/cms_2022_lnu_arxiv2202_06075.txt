Title: Search for new physics in the lepton plus missing transverse momentum final state in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
Authors: CMS Collaboration
Journal reference: JHEP 07 (2022) 067
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP07(2022)067
Report number: CMS-EXO-19-017, CERN-EP-2021-142

Abstract/result extract:
A search for physics beyond the Standard Model in final states with an
electron or muon and missing transverse momentum is presented. The
analysis uses proton-proton collision data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV collected
with the CMS detector in 2016-2018, corresponding to 138 fb^{-1}. No
significant deviation from the Standard Model prediction is observed.
Model-independent limits are set on the production cross section of W'
bosons decaying into lepton-plus-neutrino final states. In the sequential
standard model, combining electron and muon decay channels excludes a W'
boson with mass less than 5.7 TeV at 95% confidence level. Results on a
Standard Model precision test, the oblique electroweak W parameter, are
also presented using LHC data.

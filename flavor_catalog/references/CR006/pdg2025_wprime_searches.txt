Title: W' Boson Searches
Citation context: S. Navas et al. (Particle Data Group), Review of Particle Physics, 2025 update.

Relevant PDG 2025 text extract, W' -> e nu / mu nu:

"In the e nu channel, the ATLAS and CMS collaborations set limits on the
W' production cross section times branching fraction sigma x B (and thus
indirectly on the W' couplings). These limits are set for M_W' in the
0.15 - 7 TeV range and are based on approximately 140 fb^-1 at sqrt(s) =
13 TeV [18,21], with the most stringent limits reproduced in Fig. 85.1.
ATLAS sets the strongest mass limit M_W' > 6.0 TeV in the Sequential SM
(all limits in this mini-review are at the 95% CL)."

"In the mu nu channel, ATLAS and CMS set rate limits for M_W' in the
0.15 - 7 TeV range [18,21], with the strongest mass lower limit of
5.6 TeV in the Sequential SM set by CMS [21] using 138 fb^-1 of
sqrt(s) = 13 TeV data, as shown in Fig. 85.1. When combined with the
e nu channel assuming lepton universality, the upper limit on the
sqrt(s) = 13 TeV cross section times branching fraction to l nu varies
between 0.05 and 2.1 fb for M_W' values between 1 and 6 TeV [18]."

Relevant PDG 2025 model-dependence text extract:

"The tbbar channel is particularly important because a W' boson that
couples only to right-handed fermions cannot decay to leptons when the
right-handed neutrinos are heavier than M_W'. Additional motivations are
provided by a W' boson with enhanced couplings to the third generation
[12], and by a leptophobic W' boson."

Title:
Searches for lepton-flavour-violating decays of the Higgs boson into e tau
and mu tau in sqrt(s) = 13 TeV pp collisions with the ATLAS detector

Authors: ATLAS Collaboration
arXiv: 2302.05225v2
Submitted: 2023-02-10; last revised: 2023-08-23
Journal reference: JHEP 07 (2023) 166
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP07(2023)166

Relevant abstract extract:

The searches use pp collision data collected by ATLAS at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV,
corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 138 fb^-1. Leptonic and hadronic
tau decays are considered. No significant excess of events is observed, and
the results are interpreted as upper limits on lepton-flavour-violating Higgs
branching ratios.

The observed (expected) 95% CL upper limits obtained with the MC-template
method from a simultaneous measurement are:
  B(H -> e tau) < 0.20% (0.12%).
  B(H -> mu tau) < 0.18% (0.09%).

The best-fit branching-ratio difference measured with the Symmetry method in
the channel where the tau lepton decays to leptons is:
  B(H -> mu tau) - B(H -> e tau) = (0.25 +/- 0.10)%,
compatible with zero within 2.5 sigma.

Minimal extract from "11. Status of Higgs Boson Physics", section 11.4.3.2
"Flavor violating decays" (pdftotext extraction streamed 2026-05-16; no
publisher PDF saved):

The review states that the lepton-flavor-violating decays h -> e mu,
h -> e tau, and h -> mu tau are forbidden in the Standard Model but can arise
in BSM scenarios, including 2HDM and composite-Higgs models. These decays probe
off-diagonal Yukawa couplings Y_e_mu, Y_e_tau, and Y_mu_tau.

The full 13 TeV Run-2 ATLAS and CMS datasets set 95% CL upper limits on LFV
Higgs branching ratios. For h -> e tau, the corresponding observed (expected)
limits are:
  B(h -> e tau) < 0.20% (0.12%) from ATLAS [439].
  B(h -> e tau) < 0.22% (0.16%) from CMS [440].

The same paragraph gives h -> mu tau limits and notes that direct ATLAS/CMS
limits on Br(h -> mu tau) and Br(h -> e tau) are more than an order of
magnitude stronger than limits derived from tau decays.

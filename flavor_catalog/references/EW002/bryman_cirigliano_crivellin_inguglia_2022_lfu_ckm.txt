Values used in EW002:
- The review defines Delta_CKM = |Vud|^2 + |Vus|^2 + |Vub|^2 - 1.
- In practice |Vub|^2 < 1e-5 can be neglected in the low-energy discussion.
- Nuclear 0+ -> 0+ decays give Vud = 0.97373(31) in the review's summary.
- Neutron-decay comparison gives Vud = 0.97338(47) with the PDG lifetime and
  axial coupling choices used there.
- The review quotes Vus = 0.2231(6) from K_l3 decays and
  Vus/Vud = 0.2313(5) from the K_mu2/pi_mu2 ratio input available then.
- A global fit gives Vud = 0.97357(27), Vus = 0.22406(34), and
  Delta_CKM = (-19.5 +/- 5.3)e-4.

Relevance notes:
- The review stresses that beta decays, kaon decays, tau decays, and CKM
  unitarity are intertwined with tests of lepton-flavor universality and
  charged-current new physics.

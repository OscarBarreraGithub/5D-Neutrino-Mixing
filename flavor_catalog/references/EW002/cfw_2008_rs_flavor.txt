Values and context used in EW002:
- The paper re-examined flavor constraints in standard Randall-Sundrum-type
  scenarios and found a generic KK-gluon mass bound of about 21 TeV.
- In the composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs variant, extra flavor-violation
  sources and gauge-sized Yukawa couplings raised the quoted KK-gluon scale
  to about 33 TeV.
- The paper's main constraint focus is neutral-current flavor violation and
  RS-GIM suppression, not the modern first-row CKM unitarity/Cabibbo-angle
  anomaly.

Use in EW002:
- EW002's post-2008 subsection treats CFW as the requested baseline and then
  highlights that the Cabibbo anomaly became a sharper precision charged-
  current issue only after later beta-decay radiative-correction, kaon,
  lattice, and SMEFT/global-fit developments.

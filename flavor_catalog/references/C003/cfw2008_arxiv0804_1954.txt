Abstract excerpt:
The authors study the flavor structure of 5D warped models that provide a
dual description of a composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs.  They re-examine
flavor constraints on the mass scale of new physics in standard
Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios and find that the KK gluon mass should
generically be heavier than about 21 TeV.  They compare the flavor structure
of composite Higgs models to the RS model and find new contributions to
flavor violation, still suppressed by the RS-GIM mechanism, but enhancing
flavor-violating amplitudes.  In the pseudo-Goldstone scenario, Yukawa
couplings set by a gauge coupling imply a KK gluon mass of at least about
33 TeV.  The abstract concludes that fully anarchic warped flavor is
implausible without additional partial flavor symmetries.

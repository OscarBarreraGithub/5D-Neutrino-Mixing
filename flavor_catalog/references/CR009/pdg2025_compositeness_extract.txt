Relevant PDG 2025 extract, section on contact-interaction limits:

The PDG review summarizes LHC limits on llqq contact-interaction scales
from high-mass dielectron and dimuon data. It states that ATLAS, using a
data-driven background estimate, obtains combined dielectron+dimuon 95% CL
lower limits on Lambda of:

- right-right model: 35.5 TeV for constructive interference, 26.5 TeV for
  destructive interference;
- left-left model: 35.8 TeV for constructive interference, 26.0 TeV for
  destructive interference;
- left-right model: 32.5 TeV for constructive interference, 28.8 TeV for
  destructive interference.

The same paragraph states that CMS, using a 140 fb^-1 13 TeV dataset, sets
95% CL exclusion limits on llqq contact-interaction scales ranging from
Lambda_LL > 23.9 TeV for destructive interference to Lambda_RR > 36.4 TeV
for constructive interference.

Interpretation note for CR009:
These are measured exclusion limits. The contact-interaction normalization,
helicity structure, and interference sign are model assumptions attached to
the measurement and are recorded in the sidecar. Dataset luminosities and
other run metadata are kept in supporting_measurements rather than promoted
as separate pdg_or_equivalent observables.

1. arXiv:1706.03783
Title: Compilation of low-energy constraints on 4-fermion operators in the
SMEFT
Authors: Adam Falkowski, Martin Gonzalez-Alonso, Kin Mimouni
Journal reference: JHEP 08 (2017) 123
Abstract extract:
The paper compiles low-energy observables sensitive to flavor-conserving
four-fermion operators with two or four leptons, including e+e- collider
data, neutrino scattering, atomic parity violation, parity-violating
electron scattering, and pion, neutron, nuclear, and tau decays. The data
are recast as tree-level SMEFT constraints on dimension-six four-fermion
operators.

2. arXiv:1811.12260
Title: SMEFT and the Drell-Yan Process at High Energy
Authors: S. Dawson, P. P. Giardino, A. Ismail
Abstract extract:
The paper treats high-energy Drell-Yan production as an EFT probe of heavy
new physics. At tree level, four-fermion interactions dominate, while
operators modifying triple-gauge-boson couplings enter at loop level with
energy-enhanced effects. It studies sensitivity of neutral-current
Drell-Yan to dimension-six operators and compares this to WW production.

3. arXiv:1609.08157
Title: Energy helps accuracy: electroweak precision tests at hadron
colliders
Authors: Marco Farina, Giuliano Panico, Duccio Pappadopulo, Joshua T.
Ruderman, Riccardo Torre, Andrea Wulzer
Abstract extract:
The paper explains why high-energy Drell-Yan measurements can act as
electroweak precision tests. Dimension-six SMEFT operators alter the
high-energy behavior of electroweak gauge-boson propagators, and existing
8 TeV neutral-current Drell-Yan measurements have sensitivity comparable
to LEP in the studied directions.

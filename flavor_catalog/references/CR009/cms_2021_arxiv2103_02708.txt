arXiv:2103.02708
Title: Search for resonant and nonresonant new phenomena in high-mass
dilepton final states at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
Authors: CMS Collaboration
Journal reference: JHEP 07 (2021) 208
Report numbers: CMS-EXO-19-019, CERN-EP-2021-026

Abstract extract:
CMS searches for beyond-Standard-Model effects using high invariant-mass
electron or muon pairs in 13 TeV pp data collected from 2016 to 2018,
corresponding to up to 140 fb^-1. No significant deviation from the SM
background expectation is found. The analysis sets limits on narrow
resonances, Randall-Sundrum gravitons, dark-matter mediators,
four-fermion contact interactions, ADD large-extra-dimension parameters,
and a lepton-flavor-universality ratio at high mass.

Result extract:
For four-fermion contact interactions, the CMS paper reports observed lower
limits on the ultraviolet cutoff parameter Lambda that range from 23.8 to
36.4 TeV, depending on helicity structure and the sign of interference with
the SM Drell-Yan background. The main text states a range of 23.9 to
36.4 TeV and notes an improvement over the previous CMS result by
3.5--4.5 TeV.

Title: Natural Neutrino Masses and Mixings from Warped Geometry
Authors: Gilad Perez, Lisa Randall
Journal reference: JHEP01(2009)077
Submission history: submitted 30 May 2008; last revised 28 Jan 2009.

Relevant abstract text, normalized:

The authors demonstrate that flavor symmetries in warped geometry can provide a
natural explanation for large mixing angles and the distinction between quark
and lepton flavor sectors.

The model requires lepton minimal flavor violation, in which only Yukawa
matrices on the IR brane break the flavor symmetries. With this structure a
GIM-like mechanism prevents dangerous charged-lepton flavor-changing processes,
and the theory can be consistent with a cutoff of 10 TeV and 3 TeV
Kaluza-Klein masses.

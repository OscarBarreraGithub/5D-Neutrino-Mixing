Title: Z lepton flavour violation as a probe for new physics at future e+e-
colliders
Authors: Lorenzo Calibbi, Xabier Marcano, Joydeep Roy
Submission history: submitted 21 Jul 2021; last revised 13 Nov 2021.

Relevant abstract text, normalized:

The authors assess the potential of discovering new physics by searching for
lepton-flavour-violating decays of the Z boson, Z -> l_i l_j, at proposed
circular e+e- colliders CEPC and FCC-ee. Both projects plan to run at the
Z-pole as a Tera Z factory, collecting O(10^12) Z decays.

They revisit LFV Z decays in Standard Model effective field theory and study
indirect constraints from LFV mu and tau decays on operators that can induce
Z -> l_i l_j. They find that Z -> mu e rates are beyond expected sensitivities,
while a Tera Z factory is promising for Z -> tau ell decays, probing new
physics at the same level as future low-energy LFV observables.

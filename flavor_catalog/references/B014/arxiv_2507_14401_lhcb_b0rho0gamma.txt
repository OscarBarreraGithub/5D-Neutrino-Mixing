Title: Measurement of the B0 -> rho(770)0 gamma branching fraction
Authors: LHCb collaboration, R. Aaij et al.
Published: 2025-07-18
Journal reference: JHEP 12 (2025) 151

Abstract-page value extract:
  Data sample: proton-proton collision data at 7, 8, and 13 TeV, integrated luminosity 9 fb^-1.
  Ratio:
    B(B0 -> rho(770)0 gamma) / B(B0 -> K*(892)0 gamma)
      = 0.0189 +/- 0.0007 +/- 0.0005.
  Branching fraction:
    B(B0 -> rho(770)0 gamma) = (7.9 +/- 0.3 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.2) x 10^-7.
  First uncertainty is statistical, second systematic, and last from the normalisation mode.
  The result assumes that rho(770)0 and K*(892)0 decays saturate the dihadron mass spectra considered in the analysis.
  The abstract describes the result as consistent with the current world average and by far the most precise measurement to date.

Role for B014: post-2024 hadron-collider update and most precise single B0 -> rho0 gamma measurement available in the source set.

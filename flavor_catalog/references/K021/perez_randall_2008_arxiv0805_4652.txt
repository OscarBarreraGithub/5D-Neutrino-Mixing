Title: Natural Neutrino Masses and Mixings from Warped Geometry.
Authors: Gilad Perez and Lisa Randall.
Journal reference: JHEP 01 (2009) 077.
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2009/01/077.

Abstract excerpt, ASCII-normalized:
The paper shows how warped geometry can explain large lepton mixing angles and
generate Majorana neutrino masses from a UV-broken gauged U(1)_{B-L}. It uses
lepton minimal flavor violation, with Yukawa matrices as the flavor-breaking
sources, so dangerous charged-lepton flavor-changing processes are suppressed.
The abstract states that the theory can be consistent with a cutoff of 10 TeV
and 3 TeV Kaluza-Klein masses.

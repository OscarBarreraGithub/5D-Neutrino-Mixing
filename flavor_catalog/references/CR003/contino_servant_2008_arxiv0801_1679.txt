Title: Discovering the top partners at the LHC using same-sign dilepton final

Journal reference: JHEP 0806:026, 2008
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/06/026

Abstract extract:
A natural, non-supersymmetric solution to the hierarchy problem generically
requires fermionic partners of the top quark with masses not much heavier
than 500 GeV. The paper studies pair production and detection at the LHC of
top partners with electric charge Q=5/3 (T_5/3) and Q=-1/3 (B), predicted in
models where the Higgs is a pseudo-Goldstone boson.

The exotic T_5/3 fermion is identified as a distinct prediction of LR
custodial parity invariance of the electroweak-symmetry-breaking sector. Both
new fermions decay to Wt, leading to a ttbarWW final state. The paper focuses
on the same-sign-dilepton channel and states that discovery could come with
less than 100 pb^-1 for M=500 GeV and less than 20 fb^-1 for M=1 TeV.

CR003 use: theoretical baseline for custodial top-partner LHC searches. The
assigned CR003 particle is the charge-2/3 vector-like T, but the custodial
partner collider logic and same-sign/multiple-top search history are adjacent
to this originating top-partner program.

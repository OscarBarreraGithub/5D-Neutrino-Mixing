Title: First exploratory calculation of the long-distance contributions to the
rare kaon decays K -> pi l+ l-
Authors: Norman H. Christ, Xu Feng, Andreas Juettner, Andrew Lawson, Antonin
Portelli, Christopher T. Sachrajda

Relevant abstract extraction:
Rare K -> pi l+ l- decays proceed through flavor-changing neutral currents and
are naturally suppressed, making them sensitive to possible new physics.  The
CP-conserving channels contain sizable long-distance hadronic contributions,
described as a major source of theoretical uncertainty.  The paper outlines a
lattice-QCD method for computing long-distance rare-kaon amplitudes and reports
exploratory RBC/UKQCD domain-wall-fermion results.

Use in K009 catalog:
This is a post-2008 development toward first-principles control of
long-distance K -> pi l+ l- amplitudes.  It does not supersede the PDG/KTeV
experimental upper limit.

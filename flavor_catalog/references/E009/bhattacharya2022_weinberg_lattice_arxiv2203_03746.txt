Abstract-level statement:
  The authors present neutron-EDM results for dimension-4 and dimension-6
  gluonic CP violation and the isovector quark chromo-EDM, using clover
  valence quarks on HISQ dynamical ensembles generated by MILC.

Gradient-flow statement:
  For gluonic operators, the paper uses the gradient-flow scheme to obtain
  divergence-free continuum results.

Weinberg-operator mixing statement:
  The Weinberg three-gluon operator mixes with other same-dimension operators
  and with lower-dimension operators such as the topological term. In the
  gradient-flow scheme, the flow time provides a gauge- and Lorentz-symmetric
  hard ultraviolet cutoff; the matrix elements have a finite continuum limit
  but retain flow-time-dependent mixings that must be controlled.

Use in E009:
  Post-2008 lattice-QCD development showing that a production implementation
  would need nontrivial scheme, mixing, and matrix-element choices.

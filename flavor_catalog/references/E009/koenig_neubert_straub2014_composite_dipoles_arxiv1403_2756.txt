Relevance to warped/composite flavor:
  The abstract states that partial-compositeness scenarios can be seen as the
  low-energy limit of composite Higgs or warped extra dimensional models, and
  that neutron EDM measurements strongly constrain models with
  wrong-chirality Yukawa couplings.

Neutron EDM input used in that analysis:
  |d_n| < 2.9 x 10^-26 e cm at 90% CL.

Weinberg-operator threshold statement:
  In the QCD evolution of heavy-quark CEDMs to low energies, integrating out a
  heavy quark generates a finite threshold correction to the three-gluon
  Weinberg operator, which directly contributes to the neutron EDM and mixes
  under renormalization with first-generation quark (C)EDMs.

Composite/warped conclusion:
  In anarchic flavor and CP structures, the neutron EDM gives a stringent
  constraint; for average Yukawa and wrong-chirality Yukawa couplings of order
  one, the average fermion-resonance mass scale should be above about 4 TeV.

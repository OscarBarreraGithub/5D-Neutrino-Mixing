Title: Flavor Violating Higgs Decays
Authors: Roni Harnik, Joachim Kopp, Jure Zupan
arXiv: 1209.1397v3
Published: 2012-09-06; last revised: 2015-08-14
DOI: 10.1007/JHEP03(2013)026

Relevant abstract excerpt:

The paper studies flavor-violating Higgs couplings to leptons and quarks for
the newly discovered 125 GeV Higgs-like resonance. Such interactions can
arise in two-Higgs-doublet models, extra dimensions, or compositeness.

The authors rederive constraints from rare decays, electric and magnetic
dipole moments, and meson oscillations. They find that flavor-violating Higgs
decays to leptons can be sizeable; h -> tau mu and h -> tau e branching
ratios of order 10% can be allowed by low-energy constraints. They also find
that LHC limits from tau-tau recasts were already stronger than rare-tau
decay bounds and motivate dedicated h -> tau mu and h -> tau e searches.

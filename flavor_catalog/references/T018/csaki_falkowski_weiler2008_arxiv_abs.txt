Title: The Flavor of the Composite Pseudo-Goldstone Higgs
Authors: Csaba Csaki, Adam Falkowski, Andreas Weiler
arXiv: 0804.1954v2
Published: 2008-04-14; last revised: 2008-04-24
Journal reference: JHEP 0809:008, 2008
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2008/09/008

Relevant abstract excerpt:

The authors study the flavor structure of 5D warped models that provide a
dual description of a composite pseudo-Goldstone Higgs. They re-examine
flavor constraints on the mass scale of new physics in standard
Randall-Sundrum-type scenarios and find that the KK gluon mass should
generically be heavier than about 21 TeV. In the composite-Higgs models
considered, new flavor-violation contributions and gauge-sized Yukawa
couplings imply a KK gluon mass of at least about 33 TeV, with the conclusion
that fully anarchic warped flavor is implausible without additional flavor
symmetries.

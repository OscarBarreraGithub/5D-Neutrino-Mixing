Title: Natural Neutrino Masses and Mixings from Warped Geometry
Authors: Gilad Perez, Lisa Randall
arXiv: 0805.4652v3
Published: 2008-05-30; last revised: 2009-01-28
Journal reference: JHEP01(2009)077
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2009/01/077

Relevant abstract excerpt:

The paper demonstrates that flavor symmetries in warped geometry can provide
a natural explanation for large mixing angles and distinguish the quark and
lepton flavor sectors. It assumes a gauged U(1)_{B-L} symmetry broken in the
UV and lepton minimal flavor violation, with Yukawa matrices on the IR brane
as the flavor-symmetry-breaking sources. In this setup a GIM-like mechanism
prevents dangerous charged-lepton flavor-changing processes and permits
Kaluza-Klein masses near 3 TeV with a cutoff near 10 TeV.

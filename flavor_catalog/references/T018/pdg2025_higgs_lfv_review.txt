Minimal extract from "11. Status of Higgs Boson Physics", section 11.4.3.2
"Flavor violating decays" (pdftotext extraction performed 2026-05-16):

The lepton-flavor-violating decays h -> e mu, h -> e tau, and h -> mu tau
are forbidden in the Standard Model but can arise in BSM scenarios including
2HDM models and composite Higgs models. These LFV Higgs decays probe
off-diagonal Yukawa couplings Ye mu, Ye tau, or Ymu tau.

The full 13 TeV Run-2 datasets collected by ATLAS and CMS set 95% CL upper
limits on LFV Higgs branching ratios. For h -> mu tau, the observed
(expected) limits are:
  B(h -> mu tau) < 0.18% (0.09%) from ATLAS [439].
  B(h -> mu tau) < 0.15% (0.15%) from CMS [440].

The same paragraph states that direct ATLAS/CMS limits on Br(h -> mu tau) and
Br(h -> e tau) are more than an order of magnitude stronger than limits
derived from tau decays.

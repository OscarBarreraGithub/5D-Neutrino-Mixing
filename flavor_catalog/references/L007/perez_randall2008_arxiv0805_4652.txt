Title: Natural Neutrino Masses and Mixings from Warped Geometry
Authors: Gilad Perez and Lisa Randall
Journal: JHEP 01 (2009) 077
DOI: 10.1088/1126-6708/2009/01/077
Submitted: 2008-05-30; last revised: 2009-01-28

Relevant excerpt:

The paper demonstrates that flavor symmetries in warped geometry can explain
large mixing angles and distinguish quark and lepton flavor sectors. It uses
lepton minimal flavor violation, where only Yukawa matrices on the IR brane
break flavor symmetries. In this setup a GIM-like mechanism suppresses
charged-lepton flavor-changing processes, allowing consistency with a cutoff
of 10 TeV and 3 TeV Kaluza-Klein masses under the stated assumptions.

Title:
Towards B -> X_s gamma at the NNLO in QCD without interpolation in m_c

Authors:
M. Misiak, A. Rehman, M. Steinhauser

Related DOI:
10.1007/JHEP06(2020)175

Relevant excerpt:

Strengthening constraints on new physics from the B -> X_s gamma branching
ratio requires improving accuracy in the measurements and the Standard Model
predictions. To match the expected Belle-II accuracy, Next-to-Next-to-Leading
Order (NNLO) QCD corrections must be calculated without the so-far employed
interpolation in the charm-quark mass m_c.

Taking into account the recently improved estimates of non-perturbative
contributions, we find

B_s gamma = (3.40 +/- 0.17) x 10^-4

and

R_gamma = B_(s+d) gamma / B_c l nu = (3.35 +/- 0.16) x 10^-3

for E_gamma > 1.6 GeV in the decaying meson rest frame.

Extraction note:
The B011 draft uses the first number above as the current post-2015 SM
prediction for the same photon-energy threshold as the HFLAV average.

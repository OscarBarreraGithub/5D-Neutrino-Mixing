Relevant excerpt:

The observed radiative penguin branching fractions can constrain a large class
of SM extensions. However, due to the uncertainties in the hadronization, only
the inclusive b -> s gamma rate can be reliably compared with theoretical
calculations. This rate can be measured from the endpoint of the inclusive
photon spectrum in B decay. By combining the measurements of B -> X_s gamma
from the CLEO, BaBar, and Belle experiments, HFLAV obtains the average:

B(B -> X_s gamma) = (3.49 +/- 0.19) x 10^-4

for E_gamma >= 1.6 GeV, averaging over B+ and B0.

The measured branching fraction can be compared to theoretical calculations.
Recent calculations of B(b -> s gamma) at NNLO level predict for the
E_gamma >= 1.6 GeV values of

(3.36 +/- 0.23) x 10^-4 for b -> s gamma

and

(1.68 +/- 0.17) x 10^-5 for b -> d gamma decays.

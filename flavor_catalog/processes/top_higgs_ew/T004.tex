\section{\texorpdfstring{\(t\to u\gamma\)}{t -> u gamma} FCNC Top Decay}

\subsection*{Process}
\textbf{Standard notation:} \(\mathcal{B}(t\to u\gamma)\).
This entry catalogs the flavor-changing neutral-current top decay
\(t\to u\gamma\), process id T004 in the top/Higgs/electroweak family.  It
covers direct searches for an anomalous photon coupling between a top quark and
an up quark, including FCNC single-top-plus-photon production and \(t\bar t\)
events with one FCNC top decay.

\subsection*{PDG value}
The \texttt{pdg\_or\_equivalent} block in \texttt{T004.yaml} records both the
PDG combined row and flavor-separated primary limits.  The PDG 2026 top-quark
listing gives a combined
\(t\to\gamma q\), \(q=u,c\), branching-fraction limit
\[
  \mathcal{B}(t\to\gamma q) < 9.5\times 10^{-6}
\]
at 95\% CL (\texttt{PDG2026:T004:tgammaq\_combined}).  For the
flavor-separated \(t\to u\gamma\) mode, the tightest primary limit found is the
ATLAS result:
\[
  \mathcal{B}(t\to u\gamma) < 0.85\times 10^{-5}
\]
for the left-handed \(tq\gamma\) benchmark and
\(\mathcal{B}(t\to u\gamma)<1.2\times10^{-5}\) for the right-handed benchmark,
both at 95\% CL (\texttt{ATLAS2023:T004:tugamma\_left},
\texttt{ATLAS2023:T004:tugamma\_right}).  CMS later reported
\(\mathcal{B}(t\to u\gamma)<0.95\times10^{-5}\), with
\(1.20\times10^{-5}\) expected, at 95\% CL
(\texttt{CMS2024:T004:tugamma\_observed}).  Local snapshots are under
\texttt{flavor\_catalog/references/T004/}.

\subsection*{Relevance to RS / anarchic flavor}
In warped or partially composite flavor models, non-universal fermion profiles
and mass-basis rotations can induce top FCNC couplings.  The photon channel is
a dipole-like probe, complementary to \(t\to uZ\), \(t\to ug\), and \(t\to uh\).
Compared with charm final states, T004 isolates the top-up flavor rotation and
therefore probes a different corner of the anarchic up-sector structure.

\subsection*{Post-2008 developments}
The comparison baseline is arXiv:0804.1954
(\texttt{CFW2008:T004:rs\_flavor\_context}).  Since that reference epoch,
the important change is experimental: ATLAS used \(139~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) at
\(\sqrt{s}=13~\mathrm{TeV}\), while CMS used \(138~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) at the
same energy, to set direct mode-resolved limits
(\texttt{ATLAS2023:T004:dataset}; \texttt{CMS2024:T004:dataset}).  The
high-energy single-production strategy also became a standard way to discuss
future sensitivity: the theory context in
\texttt{AguilarSaavedra2017:T004:SM\_and\_projection\_context} quotes
\(10^{-5}\)-level reach at the HL-LHC and \(10^{-6}\)-level reach at an FCC-hh
for \(t\to uZ/u\gamma\)-equivalent interactions.

\subsection*{Constraint validity / model dependence}
Experimentally this is a clean null-search upper limit; the SM rate is far
below current reach and is lower than the corresponding \(t\to c\gamma\) rate
because of CKM suppression
(\texttt{AguilarSaavedra2017:T004:SM\_and\_projection\_context}).  The
interpretation is not model-independent: ATLAS quotes separate chiral
\(tq\gamma\) tensor benchmarks, and an RS backend must choose an operator
normalization before comparing scan points to the branching-fraction limit.

\subsection*{Code coverage in this repo}
Coverage is \textbf{NO}.  The PKA reran the required catalog greps across

\(tu\gamma\), \(tq\gamma\), and top-photon FCNC search returned no observable,
Wilson coefficient, or branching-ratio implementation.

\subsection*{Implementation difficulty}
Implementation difficulty is \textbf{HIGH}.  The repo has no top-FCNC photon
dipole basis, no RS-to-\(tu\gamma\) matching convention, and no top radiative
decay-width or production-recast module.  A production backend would need those
ingredients before this limit could become a scan cut.

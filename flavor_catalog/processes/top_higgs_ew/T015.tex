\section{\texorpdfstring{\(Z\to e\mu\)}{Z -> e mu} LFV Decay}

\subsection*{Process}
\textbf{Standard notation:} \(\mathcal{B}(Z\to e^\pm\mu^\mp)\).
This entry covers the charged-lepton-flavor-violating on-shell \(Z\)-boson
decay into one electron and one muon, summed over charge-conjugate final
states, in the top/Higgs/electroweak family.

\subsection*{PDG / equivalent value(s)}
The source-of-truth values are in the \texttt{pdg\_or\_equivalent} block of
\texttt{T015.yaml}.  The latest experiment-equivalent headline value is the
CMS 2025 direct-search result
\[
  \mathcal{B}(Z\to e^\pm\mu^\mp) < 1.9\times 10^{-7}
\]
with an expected limit of \(2.0\times 10^{-7}\), at 95\% CL, using
\(138~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) of \(\sqrt{s}=13~\mathrm{TeV}\) data
(\texttt{CMS2025:T015:zemu\_limit}; \texttt{CMS2025:T015:dataset}).
The PDG 2025 \(Z\)-boson listing quotes the charge-summed limit
\[
  \mathcal{B}(Z\to e^\pm\mu^\mp) < 2.62\times 10^{-7}
\]
at 95\% CL (\texttt{PDG2025:T015:zemu\_limit}), matching the ATLAS Run-2
search with \(139~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=13~\mathrm{TeV}\)
(\texttt{ATLAS2023:T015:zemu\_limit}; \texttt{ATLAS2023:T015:dataset}).
For historical comparison, the earlier ATLAS Run-1 search set
\(<7.5\times 10^{-7}\) with \(20.3~\mathrm{fb}^{-1}\) at
\(\sqrt{s}=8~\mathrm{TeV}\) (\texttt{ATLAS2014:T015:zemu\_limit};
\texttt{ATLAS2014:T015:dataset}).

\subsection*{Relevance to the RS / anarchic-flavor pipeline}
Anarchic or partially composite lepton sectors can induce non-diagonal neutral
currents after rotating to charged-lepton mass eigenstates.  A direct
\(Z\to e\mu\) limit tests any effective off-diagonal \(Z e\mu\) coupling from
KK gauge mixing, zero-mode coupling non-universality, or lepton-sector flavor
symmetry breaking.  It is therefore relevant to a lepton/electroweak extension
of the present pipeline, not to the existing quark \(\Delta F=2\) scan lane.

\subsection*{Post-2008 developments}
The arXiv:0804.1954 warped-flavor baseline predates the LHC direct
\(Z\)-LFV program.  Since then ATLAS established the \(8~\mathrm{TeV}\)
\(e\mu\) search at the \(10^{-7}\) level, then improved it with the full
Run-2 data set; CMS subsequently pushed the direct \(e\mu\) limit below
\(2\times10^{-7}\).  On the theory side, post-LEP EFT studies of future
Tera-\(Z\) facilities emphasize that indirect low-energy \(\mu\)-to-\(e\)
constraints can make \(Z\to \mu e\) harder to observe than \(Z\to\tau\ell\)
even with \(\mathcal{O}(10^{12})\) produced \(Z\) bosons
(\texttt{CalibbiMarcanoRoy2021:T015:teraZ}).

\subsection*{Constraint validity and model dependence}
Experimentally this is a clean direct upper limit on a forbidden Standard
Model decay.  Its use as a scan cut assumes SM-like inclusive \(Z\) production
and total \(Z\) width.  Model interpretations are less universal: in many
lepton-flavor models the same operator structure is correlated with
\(\mu\to e\gamma\), \(\mu\to3e\), or coherent \(\mu\)-to-\(e\) conversion,
so this should be treated as a direct collider constraint on \(Z e\mu\), not
as a complete charged-lepton flavor-violation likelihood.

\subsection*{Code coverage in this repo}
Coverage is \textbf{NO}.  The required broad greps and a targeted search for
\texttt{Z -> e mu}, \texttt{Z -> mu e}, \texttt{Zemu}, and related LFV-\(Z\)
strings across \texttt{quarkConstraints/}, \texttt{qcd/},
\texttt{flavorConstraints/}, \texttt{neutrinos/}, \texttt{yukawa/},
\texttt{warpConfig/}, \texttt{solvers/}, \texttt{scanParams/}, and
\texttt{tests/} found no \(Z\to e\mu\) observable implementation.  The only
charged-lepton LFV backend seen is \(\mu\to e\gamma\)
(\texttt{flavorConstraints/muToEGamma.py:75}), wired into the scan at
\texttt{scanParams/scan.py:524}; the modern phenomenology policy surface is
limited to \(\epsilon_K\), \(K\), \(B_d\), \(B_s\), and \(D^0\)
(\texttt{quarkConstraints/modern/phenomenology.py:23}).

\subsection*{Implementation difficulty}
Production integration is \textbf{HIGH}.  Storing the external limit is
straightforward, but a faithful RS constraint needs a new electroweak LFV
operator convention, matching from lepton localization and gauge/Higgs-sector
mixing to \(Z e\mu\), and a decision on how to combine direct \(Z\)-pole
limits with low-energy \(\mu\)-flavor observables.

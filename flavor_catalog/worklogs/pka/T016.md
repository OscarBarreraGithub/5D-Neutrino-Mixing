# PKA Worklog: T016 Z -> e tau

I followed the required reading sequence and used process row T016 in the
top/Higgs/electroweak family.  Search queries included "PDG 2025 Z boson
listing Z e tau", "ATLAS Search for Lepton-Flavor Violation in Z-Boson Decays
with tau leptons 5.0 10^-6", "CMS charged lepton flavor violating Z e tau
13.8 11.4 10^-6", and "Z lepton flavour violation future e+e- colliders".

I used the PDG 2025 Z-boson listing as the official listing snapshot and the
ATLAS 2021 PRL/arXiv result as the primary experiment behind the listed
charge-summed limit.  The latest independent LHC search I found is CMS 2025,
which postdates the PDG listing but is weaker in the e-tau channel, so it is
recorded as a post-2008 cross-check rather than the canonical limit.  I also
kept the ATLAS Nature Physics hadronic-tau result because the 2021 PRL combines
against previously published analyses and the earlier paper documents the
hadronic-tau input.  Calibbi-Marcano-Roy 2021 was included for Tera-Z
interpretation, and arXiv:0804.1954 plus arXiv:0805.4652 were included as
paper-era RS/lepton-sector context.

No publisher PDFs were committed.  The PDG material is a short normalized text
extract containing only the LFV Z rows and footnote.  All experiment/theory
sources are arXiv metadata/abstract snapshots; each text file is under 100 KB.

Code coverage was checked with the three required greps over `quarkConstraints`,
`qcd`, `flavorConstraints`, `neutrinos`, `yukawa`, `warpConfig`, `solvers`,
`scanParams`, and `tests`, plus a targeted Z-e-tau grep.  The targeted grep
returned no hits.  Existing charged-lepton LFV code is limited to
`mu -> e gamma`, and the modern phenomenology policy surface remains the
neutral-meson Delta F=2 set.

Unresolved issue for WA/CA: decide how tightly this direct Z-pole bound should
be correlated with low-energy tau LFV constraints in future lepton-sector
integration.  For this PKA draft it is cataloged only as a direct collider
bound on an off-diagonal Z e tau coupling.

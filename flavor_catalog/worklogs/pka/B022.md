# PKA Worklog: B022

Process: `B -> K nu nubar invisible` (`B022`, beauty).

I first checked out `flavor-catalog/2026q2` and read the required planning,
orchestrator, Opus signoff, process-template, and README files.  The branch
already had unrelated dirty files in other process IDs, which I left untouched.

Source search focused on the current experimental value and post-2008 changes.
Searches included `Belle II 2024 B+ -> K+ nu nu branching fraction 2.7 sigma`,
`PDG B+ -> K+ nu nubar branching fraction`, `HFLAV rare B B+ K+ nu nubar`,
and `BaBar B -> K(*) nu nubar arXiv`.  The useful primary/equivalent sources
were HFLAV Dec. 2025, PDG live/API listing `S041.273`, the Belle II PubDB page
for Phys. Rev. D 109, 112006 (2024), BaBar arXiv:1303.7465, HPQCD
arXiv:2207.13371 for the SM prediction, Belle II's 2025 model-agnostic
likelihood PubDB page, and arXiv:0804.1954 as the paper-era RS-flavor baseline.
I avoided publisher PDFs and stored only minimal text/API/HTML snapshots or a
short arXiv TeX excerpt under `flavor_catalog/references/B022/`.

Canonical value decision: I used HFLAV Dec. 2025 as the headline averaged
experimental value, \((1.38 \pm 0.35)\times 10^{-5}\), while also recording
PDG live/Belle II because the Belle II 2024 evidence measurement is the central
post-2008 physics point: \((2.3 \pm 0.5_{\rm stat}{}^{+0.5}_{-0.4}{}_{\rm syst})
\times 10^{-5}\), 3.5 sigma evidence, 2.7 sigma above SM.  The HPQCD SM number
\((5.58 \pm 0.37)\times 10^{-6}\) is kept under theory inputs rather than as a
`pdg_or_equivalent` observable.

`flavorConstraints/`, `neutrinos/`, `yukawa/`, `warpConfig/`, `solvers/`,

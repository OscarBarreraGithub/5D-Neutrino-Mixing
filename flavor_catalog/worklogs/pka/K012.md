# PKA Worklog: K012

Process: `K_S^0 -> mu+ mu-` rare leptonic kaon decay.

I first checked the required plan, orchestrator decisions, Opus signoff, process
template, and catalog README.  The assigned row identifies K012 as a kaon-sector
LHCb upper-limit process with initial coverage `NOT-SEEN`.

Source search focused on the current experimental limit and post-2008 changes.
Useful queries included `K_S -> mu+ mu- LHCb upper limit branching fraction`,
`PDG K_S mu+ mu- branching fraction limit`, and `Constraints on the K0S mu+mu-
Branching Fraction arXiv`.  The canonical value was taken from the PDG 2025
`K0S` listing: `BR(K_S^0 -> mu+ mu-) < 2.1e-10` at 90% CL.  I used
`pdftotext` on a temporary PDG PDF copy and saved only a short text excerpt in
`flavor_catalog/references/K012/pdg2025_ks_decay_modes.txt`; no PDG or publisher
PDF is tracked.  Primary provenance comes from LHCb arXiv:2001.10354, whose
abstract gives the 2016-2018 standalone `2.2e-10` limit and the combined
`2.1e-10` limit, and from the earlier LHCb arXiv:1706.00758 Run-1 limit.

For theory context I saved compact arXiv abstract snapshots for Chobanova et al.
2018, giving the Standard Model scale near `5e-12`, and Dery-Ghosh-Grossman-
Schacht 2021, giving the time-dependent clean `ell=0` proposal and the quoted
sub-1% hadronic uncertainty.  Springer/publisher pages were used only as
discovery aids and were not tracked.

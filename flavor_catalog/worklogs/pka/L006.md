# PKA Worklog: L006 muonium-antimuonium conversion

I checked out `flavor-catalog/2026q2` first and read the required plan,
orchestrator decisions, Opus signoff, process template, and catalog README in
order. The assignment row is L006 in `charged_lepton`, described as
muonium-antimuonium conversion with initial coverage `NOT-SEEN`.

Source searches included `PDG muonium antimuonium conversion S004MC`,
`Willmann 1999 muonium antimuonium 8.3e-11`, `MACE muonium antimuonium
conversion sensitivity`, `Snowmass2021 muonium to antimuonium conversion`, and
`Delta L_mu Delta L_e two units muonium antimuonium EFT`. I used PDG pdgLive
S004MC as the canonical current source. It quotes `G_C/G_F < 0.0030` at 90% CL
under the stated `(V-A)x(V-A)` convention and notes the Willmann/MACS
probability limit `P_M Mbar < 8.3e-11` at 90% CL in a 0.1 T field. I kept a
minimal MACS/Willmann snapshot from arXiv/final metadata; no APS publisher PDF
was saved. One source caveat is that the arXiv abstract says `8.2e-11`, while
PDG and final article metadata support `8.3e-11`; I followed PDG.

For post-2008 developments relative to arXiv:0804.1954, I retained the
Snowmass 2021 muonium whitepaper, the 2024 MACE conceptual design report, and
the 2024 Heeck-Sokhashvili EFT paper on lepton flavor violation by two units.
The MACE sources are prospective rather than current bounds, so they are kept
outside the `pdg_or_equivalent` block. Agashe-Blechman-Petriello 2006 is used
only for RS lepton-LFV context.

Coverage greps were rerun over `quarkConstraints`, `qcd`, `flavorConstraints`,
`neutrinos`, `yukawa`, `warpConfig`, `solvers`, `scanParams`, and `tests`. The
targeted `muonium|antimuonium|MACE|MACS` grep returned no hits. The only
adjacent charged-lepton LFV path found was `flavorConstraints/muToEGamma.py`
and its scan call, so L006 coverage is `NO`.

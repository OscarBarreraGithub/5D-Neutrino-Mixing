# PKA Worklog: T017 Z -> mu tau

I worked on process `T017`, family `top_higgs_ew`, for the
charged-lepton-flavor-violating decay `Z -> mu tau`.  Required governance
documents were read in order before drafting.  I used the PDG 2025 Z-boson
listing as the official value snapshot, then checked the primary ATLAS and CMS
LHC searches that determine or cross-check the mu-tau limit.

Searches and source checks included: `PDG 2025 Z boson listing Z -> mu tau`,
`Z -> mu tau branching fraction upper limit CMS 2025 138 fb-1 arXiv`,
`ATLAS search Z -> mu tau branching ratio limit 13 TeV 139 fb-1`, and
`Search for lepton-flavor-violation in Z-boson decays with tau-leptons
arXiv`.  The retained primary snapshots are PDG 2025, ATLAS arXiv:2105.12491,
CMS arXiv:2508.07512, Calibbi-Marcano-Roy arXiv:2107.10273 for future-Z
context, and the paper-era arXiv:0804.1954 and arXiv:0805.4652 warped-flavor
references.  I did not save publisher PDFs.  PDG and arXiv pages were reduced
to minimal local text extracts, each under 100 KB, with SHA-256 hashes recorded
in the sidecar and manifest.

The canonical observed limit recorded is the PDG/ATLAS charge-summed
`B(Z -> mu tau) < 6.5e-6` at 95% CL.  CMS 2025 is newer but gives a weaker
observed mu-tau limit, `12.0e-6`, with expected `5.3e-6`, so I retained it as
an important independent Run-2 cross-check rather than the headline observed
bound.

For code coverage, I reran the three required catalog greps plus a targeted
`Z -> mu tau`/`Z -> tau mu`/`Zmutau` search across `quarkConstraints`, `qcd`,
`flavorConstraints`, `neutrinos`, `yukawa`, `warpConfig`, `solvers`,
`scanParams`, and `tests`.  The targeted search returned no hits.  The only
charged-lepton LFV backend found is still `mu -> e gamma`, called from the scan
pipeline.  Open issues for later CA/WA review are whether future PDG listings
will mention CMS 2025 explicitly and what left/right LFV-Z coupling convention
should be used if this catalog entry graduates into code.

## Canonical Limit Choice

Selected canonical mass limit:

- `m(t'(5/3)) > 1460 GeV` at `95% CL`, from the current PDG encoder q009
  table, documenting AAD 23AG / ATLAS arXiv:2212.05263.

Why this is the canonical CR002 value:

- It is the current PDG-leading charge-5/3 pair-production mass limit.
- The ATLAS source uses full Run-2 `139 fb^-1` at `sqrt(s)=13 TeV`.
- The relevant interpretation is the exclusive `B/X -> Wt` final state, which
  matches the CR002 topology.

Supporting values captured:

- ATLAS arXiv:2212.05263 also quotes `1.59 TeV` for a mass-degenerate VLQ
  doublet benchmark; this is model-context, not the process headline.
- CMS arXiv:1810.03188 gives `1.33 TeV` and `1.30 TeV` for right- and
  left-handed X_5/3 coupling benchmarks.
- ATLAS arXiv:1807.11883 gives `1.19 TeV` for pair production, and PDG notes
  `1.6 TeV` when a unit-coupling single-production interpretation is included.
- CMS arXiv:1705.10967 gives `1.02 TeV` and `0.99 TeV`.
- CMS arXiv:1312.2391 gives the first LHC same-sign-dilepton limit,
  `800 GeV`.

## Post-2010 History

- CMS arXiv:1312.2391: first LHC same-sign-dilepton charge-5/3 top-partner
  search; important because it operationalized the Contino--Servant channel.
- CMS arXiv:1705.10967: first CMS 13 TeV combination of same-sign dilepton and
  single-lepton channels.
- ATLAS arXiv:1807.11883: same-charge lepton plus b-jet ATLAS search; includes
  both pair-production and coupling-dependent single-production interpretations.
- CMS arXiv:1810.03188: higher-luminosity CMS Run-2 search with right- and
  left-handed coupling benchmarks.
- ATLAS arXiv:2212.05263: full Run-2 ATLAS one-lepton plus MET search and the
  current PDG-leading charge-5/3 pair-production table entry.

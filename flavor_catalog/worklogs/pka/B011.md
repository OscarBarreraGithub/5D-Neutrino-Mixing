# PKA Worklog: B011

Assigned process: inclusive \(B \to X_s\gamma\) / \(b\to s\gamma\).  I
matched this to process id `B011` from plan v1 Section C and used family
`beauty`.  No ambiguity was found in the process-id choice.

Required plan documents and the process template were read first.  I then
checked HFLAV, the PDG 2024 b-hadron review, the Misiak NNLO theory papers,
and recent Belle/Belle II experimental inputs.  The canonical experimental
number I extracted is the HFLAV average
`B(B -> X_s gamma) = (3.49 +/- 0.19) x 10^-4` for `E_gamma > 1.6 GeV`.
The HFLAV Dec 2024 rare-decay table gives the same quantity as `349 +/- 19`
in units of `10^-6`, after including Belle, Belle II, BaBar, and CLEO
measurements extrapolated to the common photon-energy threshold.  The PDG
2024 b-hadron review quotes the same HFLAV experimental average and also
quotes the 2015 Misiak et al. NNLO SM prediction `(3.36 +/- 0.23) x 10^-4`.
For the current theory comparison, I recorded the 2020
Misiak/Rehman/Steinhauser update `(3.40 +/- 0.17) x 10^-4`.

I followed the licensing policy: no publisher PDFs are stored.  All local
reference files are small text snapshots under `flavor_catalog/references/B011/`
with sha256 sums in `source_manifest.yaml` and `sha256sums.txt`.

For code coverage I ran the three required catalog greps plus a focused grep
for `X_s`, `b->s gamma`, `C7`, and `C8` across implementation and test
directories.  The focused grep returned no hits.  The broader greps only found
catalog planning text and unrelated lepton-sector `mu->e gamma` dipole code.
The modern quark phenomenology surface is explicitly limited to neutral-meson
mixing systems, so I marked B011 coverage as `NO`.

Open question for WA/CA: whether the final prose should headline the
PDG-reviewed 2015 Misiak et al. theory number or the 2020 updated
Misiak/Rehman/Steinhauser number.  I recorded both and used the 2020 value as

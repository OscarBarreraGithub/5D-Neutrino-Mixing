# PKA Worklog: E008 Quark chromo-EDM bounds

I followed the required catalog plan on branch `flavor-catalog/2026q2` and
worked only under `flavor_catalog/`. The assigned process is E008 in the
`edm_neutrino` family. I treated it as an EFT-translation entry rather than a
direct PDG observable: qCEDMs are bounded through neutron and diamagnetic-atom
EDM measurements plus hadronic/nuclear matrix elements.

Searches used included "quark chromo EDM neutron mercury bounds tilde d",
"d_Hg tilde d_u tilde d_d", "Pospelov Ritz neutron chromoelectric dipole
moment", "composite Higgs neutron EDM chromomagnetic dipole", "warped wrong
chirality Yukawa neutron EDM", and "lattice isovector quark chromo-electric
dipole neutron EDM". I used PDG Live S017EDM as the current neutron anchor,
Graner et al. 2016 for the direct `199Hg` atom EDM limit, Pospelov--Ritz for
the neutron qCEDM sum-rule formula, Olive--Pospelov--Ritz--Santoso for the Hg
isovector qCEDM formula, CFW 2008 as the required RS baseline, Koenig--Neubert--
Straub 2014 for post-2008 composite/warped dipole constraints, and
Bhattacharya et al. 2024 for recent lattice treatment of the isovector qCEDM.
I saved only minimal text snapshots and source metadata; no publisher PDFs were
cached. Publisher pages, ScienceDirect/Springer pages, and full PDG PDFs were
rejected in favor of arXiv/PDG text snapshots.

For code coverage I ran focused qCEDM/neutron-EDM/mercury-EDM/Weinberg greps
and the broad catalog grep terms over `quarkConstraints`, `qcd`,
`flavorConstraints`, `neutrinos`, `yukawa`, `warpConfig`, `solvers`,
`scanParams`, and `tests`. No qCEDM or hadronic EDM implementation was found.
The only nearby matches are the unrelated `mu -> e gamma` dipole helper in
`flavorConstraints/muToEGamma.py` and generic QCD/Delta F=2 running vocabulary.
I marked code coverage `NO` and implementation difficulty `HIGH`.

Unresolved issue: the derived qCEDM numbers are central, one-source-at-a-time
translations and should not be mistaken for a correlated global EDM fit.
WA/CA should decide whether the final catalog presents them as benchmark
bounds or only as EFT translation examples.

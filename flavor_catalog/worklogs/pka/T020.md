# PKA Worklog: T020 h -> e mu Higgs LFV

I followed the required reading order: plan v1, orchestrator decisions, Opus
signoff, the LaTeX process template, and the catalog README.  The assigned row
is T020 in `top_higgs_ew`, with initial code coverage `NOT-SEEN`.

Source search focused on PDG, ATLAS, and CMS.  Queries used included
`PDG 2025 Higgs lepton flavor violation h to e mu limit`, `ATLAS h to e mu
Higgs 139 fb-1 branching fraction`, and `CMS HIG-22-002 h to e mu limit`.
The canonical aggregator is the PDG 2025 Status of Higgs Boson Physics review,
section 11.4.3.2.  I saved only a minimal text snapshot, not the PDG PDF.  PDG
quotes the full Run-2 direct limits for a 125 GeV Higgs: CMS
`B(h -> e mu) < 4.4e-5` with `4.7e-5` expected, and ATLAS
`B(h -> e mu) < 6.2e-5` with `5.9e-5` expected, both at 95% CL.

Primary source snapshots were saved for the CMS public result page
CMS-HIG-22-002 and the ATLAS arXiv abstract 1909.10235.  The CMS result is the
strongest direct limit and also documents the 110-160 GeV X -> e mu scan, with
a 146 GeV excess of local/global significance 3.8/2.8 sigma that is not
evidence.  The ATLAS abstract reports 6.1e-5 observed and 5.8e-5 expected,
slightly different from the PDG-rounded values; I flagged that for WA/CA.
Theory/context snapshots were saved for arXiv:1209.1397, arXiv:0804.1954, and
arXiv:0805.4652.

Coverage greps were run across the required code directories:
`quarkConstraints`, `qcd`, `flavorConstraints`, `neutrinos`, `yukawa`,
`warpConfig`, `solvers`, `scanParams`, and `tests`.  No h -> e mu observable or
Y_e_mu/Y_mu_e implementation was found.  The nearby implemented LFV path is
only `mu -> e gamma` in `flavorConstraints/muToEGamma.py`, applied by
`scanParams/scan.py`; `yukawa/charged_lepton.py` is diagonal mass-relation
support.  I therefore classified code coverage as NO and implementation
difficulty as HIGH.

# PKA Worklog: E006 Mercury-199 EDM

I followed the required catalog plan on branch `flavor-catalog/2026q2` and
worked only under `flavor_catalog/`.  The assigned process is the permanent
electric dipole moment of neutral `199Hg`, process id `E006` in family
`edm_neutrino`.  I treated Graner et al. 2016, arXiv:1601.04339 / PRL 116
161601, as the canonical direct experimental value source.  The arXiv v4
abstract gives `|d_Hg| < 7.4e-30 e cm` at 95% C.L., from a zero-consistent
central measurement with statistical and systematic uncertainties `2.75` and
`1.48` in units of `10^-30 e cm`.  I recorded an open checker issue because
PDG/PubMed-style references to the PRL erratum should be used before quoting
the signed central value in final prose; the absolute limit is unaffected.

Searches used included "PDG Live 199Hg EDM Graner", "PDG S017EDM 199Hg atom
EDM", "arXiv 1601.04339 mercury EDM", "Sahoo 2017 199Hg EDM Schiff moment",
and "arXiv 0804.1954 warped flavor".  I saved minimal text snapshots for the
Graner arXiv record, the PDG 2026 neutron EDM datablock cross-reference rows
that cite Hg atom EDM plus theory, Sahoo 2017 for post-2008 atomic/nuclear
translation, and the required CFW 2008 warped-flavor baseline.  I did not
cache publisher PDFs.

For post-2008 developments, I focused on the 2016 direct Hg improvement and
the 2017 theory translation, not on older Hg EDM history.  Sahoo 2017 derives
interpretation-level limits including neutron/proton EDMs, `theta_bar`, and
the up-down quark chromo-EDM difference; I marked these as model-dependent
translations rather than direct measurements.

For code coverage I ran the three required catalog greps and a focused grep
over `quarkConstraints`, `qcd`, `flavorConstraints`, `neutrinos`, `yukawa`,
`warpConfig`, `solvers`, `scanParams`, and `tests` for Hg, mercury, Schiff,
diamagnetic, EDM, chromo-EDM, Weinberg, and dipole terms.  No `199Hg` EDM
implementation was found.  The only adjacent matches are the existing
off-diagonal `mu -> e gamma` dipole helper in `flavorConstraints/muToEGamma.py`,
which is not an atomic or hadronic EDM calculation.  I therefore marked code
coverage `NO` and implementation difficulty `HIGH`.

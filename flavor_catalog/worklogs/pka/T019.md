# PKA Worklog: T019 h -> e tau

Process: T019, family `top_higgs_ew`.  I read the required planning files in
order, checked the companion T018 h -> mu tau draft, and then built an
independent T019 source set under `flavor_catalog/references/T019/`.

Searches and source checks used:
`PDG 2025 Higgs lepton flavor violating decays h e tau ATLAS CMS 0.20 0.22`,
`CMS 2021 h e tau branching fraction 0.22 0.16 arXiv 2105.03007`,
`ATLAS 2023 Higgs lepton flavour violating e tau mu tau 0.20 0.12 arXiv
2302.05225`, and `Harnik Kopp Zupan Flavor Violating Higgs Decays h tau e
order 10% arXiv 1209.1397`.  I streamed the PDG 2025 Higgs review through
`pdftotext` without saving the PDF and kept only the small relevant text
extract.  For primary experiment inputs I used the arXiv records for the
ATLAS full Run-2 search, the CMS full Run-2 search, and the earlier ATLAS
partial Run-2 search.  For theory context I kept arXiv text snapshots for
Harnik-Kopp-Zupan 2012, Csaki-Falkowski-Weiler 2008, and Perez-Randall 2008.

Canonical value selected: the PDG/equivalent source quotes full Run-2 limits
for B(h -> e tau): ATLAS observed (expected) < 0.20% (0.12%) and CMS
observed (expected) < 0.22% (0.16%) at 95% CL.  I phrase ATLAS as the
strongest observed single-experiment headline limit, while retaining the PDG
pair in the YAML sidecar.

Rejected or not tracked: publisher PDFs and journal pages, ResearchGate
mirrors, and a broad 2025 CMS conference overview because they were either
not primary for the numerical h -> e tau limit or unnecessary once the PDG,
ATLAS, and CMS primary snapshots were available.

Code coverage: the required greps over `quarkConstraints/`, `qcd/`,
`flavorConstraints/`, `neutrinos/`, `yukawa/`, `warpConfig/`, `solvers/`,
`scanParams/`, and `tests/` found no h -> e tau implementation.  The only
nearby LFV code is the existing mu -> e gamma dipole filter and diagonal
charged-lepton Yukawa helper, cited in the YAML and TeX.

Open issues for WA/CA: decide whether the prose headline should be the
strongest ATLAS limit alone or the PDG ATLAS/CMS pair, and define the future
RS mapping from off-diagonal Higgs Yukawas to a branching-ratio constraint if
this process graduates into live scan code.

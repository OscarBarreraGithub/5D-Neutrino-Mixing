# Flavor from Minimal Flavor Violation & a Viable Randall-Sundrum Model

- arXiv: [0710.1869](https://arxiv.org/abs/0710.1869)
- PDF: [0710.1869](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.1869)
- Local PDF: [`0710.1869v1.pdf`](0710.1869v1.pdf)
- Journal: Phys. Rev. Lett. 100, 171604 (2008)

## Paper thesis

The model keeps the RS flavor hierarchy mechanism, but imposes 5D MFV so that
the only flavor-breaking sources are two anarchic quark Yukawa matrices. The
bulk masses are built from those Yukawas, and the low-energy theory becomes a
next-to-MFV structure dominated by third-generation effects.

## Key numerical ideas from the paper

- `C_u,d ~ Y_u,d^\dagger Y_u,d`
- `C_Q ~ r Y_u Y_u^\dagger + Y_d Y_d^\dagger`
- `r -> 0` gives an exact down-sector flavor-suppression limit
- the paper’s numerical solutions favor `r ~ 0.1-0.4`
- a typical Yukawa size `y ~ 3` is used in the benchmark discussion
- the benchmark relation is written as `diag(C_Q) = a diag[r V_5^† C_u V_5 + C_d]`
- the paper quotes a fast proxy `h_RS ~ 0.5 * (3 TeV / m_KK)^2 * (f_Q3 / 0.3)^4`
- the resulting suppression is enough to relax the RS flavor and CP problem
  to the point that KK scales near `2 TeV` are described as viable

## Implication for this repo

This paper is not just "another constraint." It suggests a structural
alternative to the lepton-sector anarchic scan already in the repo:

- the flavor problem is no longer "generic RS is excluded unless KK is huge"
- the down-sector can be parametrically protected
- the dominant constraints may move to the up sector and to CP observables
- the central numerical question becomes the allowed `r` window consistent
  with quark masses, CKM, and FCNC suppression

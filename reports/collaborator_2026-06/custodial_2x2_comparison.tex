\textbf{The one-line answer.} Custodial symmetry is an \emph{electroweak} cure, not a
\emph{flavor} cure. It protects the oblique $T$ parameter and the $Z\,b_L\bar b_L$
vertex, pulling those floors from tens of TeV down to a few~TeV, but it leaves the
$\varepsilon_K$ wall flat at tens of TeV, because the binding kaon operator is the
color-octet KK-gluon $C_4$, an electroweak singlet the custodial group cannot touch.
This note works through that observable by observable, separates the two protections
custodial actually provides, and lists which papers we followed.

% ===================== WHAT CUSTODIAL TOUCHES =====================
\section*{1.\quad What custodial symmetry touches, and how we implemented it}

Custodial RS gauges $\mathrm{SU(2)}_L\times\mathrm{SU(2)}_R\times\mathrm{U(1)}_X$
with the discrete $P_{LR}$. It is an electroweak symmetry: it acts on the oblique
parameters and the $Z\,b\bar b$ vertex, and is blind to the color-octet KK-gluon
operator that sets $\varepsilon_K$.

% ===================== TWO PROTECTIONS =====================
\section*{2.\quad The two protections: SU(2)$_R$ protects $T$, $P_{LR}$ protects $Z b_L$}

Custodial bundles two distinct protections that do different jobs, and we can switch
them on separately. The gauge SU(2)$_R$ tames the oblique $T$ parameter; the extra
discrete $P_{LR}$ protects the $Z b_L$ vertex. Running all three models (minimal,
SU(2)$_R$ only, SU(2)$_R\!+\!P_{LR}$) gives the clean decomposition below: the
oblique floor is the largest $M_{KK}$ excluded at $95\%$ against the PDG-2025
$U$-fixed ellipse, and $\delta g_L^b$ is the $Z b_L$ vertex shift at $M_{KK}=3$~TeV.

SU(2)$_R$ alone does the entire oblique job: it pulls the $S,T,U$ floor from
$16$ down to $5.8$~TeV (the leftover is the unprotected $S$), and it leaves $Z b_L$
exactly where minimal had it. $P_{LR}$ then does the $Z b_L$ job: it collapses
$\delta g_L^b$ to zero without touching the oblique floor at all. So each symmetry
cures one observable, independently. This is why the $Z\to b\bar b$ ``no custodial''
plot below stands for \emph{both} minimal and SU(2)$_R$-only: SU(2)$_R$ does not move
those couplings, only $P_{LR}$ does.

\section*{4.\quad Which papers we followed}

\textbf{Followed (formulas taken and coded up):} the four electroweak-custodial
papers. \textbf{CGHNP} (Casagrande et al., 0807.4937): the custodial $T$ (Eq.~153),
the minimal/custodial oblique, and the $Z b\bar b$ tree couplings (Eq.~170).
\textbf{ADMS} (Agashe-Delgado-May-Sundrum, hep-ph/0308036): the custodial-$T$
mechanism. \textbf{ACDP} (Agashe-Contino-Da~Rold-Pomarol, hep-ph/0605341): the
$P_{LR}$ protection of $Z b_L$ and the $g_R$ embedding. \textbf{CPSW}
(Carena-Pont\'on-Santiago-Wagner, hep-ph/0607106 and 0701055): the top-partner one
loop.

\textbf{Did not follow (cross-checks only, nothing implemented):} the
custodial-flavor papers. \textbf{CFW} (0804.1954) and \textbf{Blanke et al.}
(0809.1073) and \textbf{Bauer~II} (0912.1625), including Bauer~II Fig.~13 / Eq.~131
(the custodial right-handed $Z$ FCNC, enhanced $\sim\!10\times$), are used only to
confirm that the $\varepsilon_K$ wall stays at tens of TeV. The split is electroweak
vs.\ flavor: we follow the papers that give the custodial effect on $S,T,U$ and
$Z b\bar b$, the floors we run into, and we do not implement the custodial flavor
effects, which stay subleading to the KK-gluon for $\varepsilon_K$. The detailed
per-paper map is \texttt{docs/CUSTODIAL\_PAPER\_MAP.pdf}.

% ===================== CONCLUSION =====================
\section*{Bottom line}

Custodial symmetry does exactly two things to what we run, and they are separable:
SU(2)$_R$ protects the oblique $T$ parameter (the $S,T,U$ floor drops from $16$ to
$5.8$~TeV, the residual set by the unprotected $S$), and the extra $P_{LR}$ protects
the $Z\,b_L\bar b_L$ vertex at tree level (top-partner loops partially undo it). It
does \textbf{nothing} to $\varepsilon_K$: the binding kaon operator is the
color-octet KK-gluon $C_4$, an electroweak singlet, so the wall stays at tens of
TeV --- in our run and in Blanke's full custodial calculation alike. Custodial is an
electroweak cure, not a flavor cure. Only flavor \emph{alignment} (RH-down U(3)
degeneracy, 5D-MFV, $V_{5KM}$) moves the $\varepsilon_K$ wall.
